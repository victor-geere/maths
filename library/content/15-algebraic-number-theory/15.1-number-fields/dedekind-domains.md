# Dedekind Domains

A **Dedekind domain** is the abstract algebraic structure that encapsulates the key arithmetic properties of rings of integers of number fields: every non-zero ideal factors uniquely into prime ideals. While the name "Dedekind domain" was coined later, the properties were identified by Richard Dedekind to explain why the ring of integers $\mathcal{O}_K$ has such good factorisation theory despite failing to be a UFD in general. Dedekind domains arise not only in algebraic number theory but also as coordinate rings of smooth affine curves in algebraic geometry. Their theory unifies and generalises Euclidean domains, PIDs, and rings of integers into a coherent framework where ideal arithmetic is the fundamental tool.

## Definition

An integral domain $R$ is a **Dedekind domain** if it satisfies all of:

1. **Noetherian:** every ideal is finitely generated
2. **Integrally closed:** if $\alpha \in \text{Frac}(R)$ satisfies a monic polynomial with coefficients in $R$, then $\alpha \in R$
3. **Krull dimension 1:** every non-zero prime ideal is maximal

Equivalently, every non-zero ideal of $R$ is uniquely a product of prime ideals.

## Examples

| Ring | Dedekind domain? |
|---|---|
| $\mathbb{Z}$ | yes (PID, so trivially) |
| $\mathcal{O}_K$ (ring of integers) | yes |
| $k[C]$ for smooth affine curve $C$ | yes |
| $\mathbb{Z}[x]$ | no (dimension 2) |
| $k[x,y]$ | no (not dim 1) |
| $\mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{-3}]$ (not the full $\mathcal{O}_K$) | no (not integrally closed) |

## Unique Factorisation of Ideals

**Theorem:** In a Dedekind domain $R$, every non-zero proper ideal $\mathfrak{a}$ factors uniquely as a product of prime ideals.

## Fractional Ideals Form a Group

In a Dedekind domain, the set of non-zero fractional ideals forms a **free abelian group** generated by the prime ideals.

## PID $\iff$ Class Number 1

A Dedekind domain is a PID $\iff$ it is a UFD $\iff$ every fractional ideal is principal $\iff h_K = 1$.

## Glossary

| Term / Symbol | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Dedekind domain | Noetherian, integrally closed, Krull dimension 1 integral domain |
| Noetherian | every ideal is finitely generated |
| Integrally closed | contains all roots of monic polynomials from its fraction field |
| Krull dimension 1 | every non-zero prime ideal is maximal |
| Integral domain | commutative ring with no zero divisors |
| $\text{Frac}(R)$ | fraction field of $R$ |
| PID | principal ideal domain: every ideal is principal |
| UFD | unique factorisation domain |
| $h_K = 1$ | class number 1 iff Dedekind domain is a PID |
| Free abelian group | $\cong \mathbb{Z}^{(I)}$ for some index set $I$; fractional ideals form this |
