# Noetherian Rings

A ring $R$ is **Noetherian** if it satisfies the **ascending chain condition (ACC)** on ideals: every chain $I_1 \subseteq I_2 \subseteq \cdots$ of ideals eventually stabilises ($I_n = I_{n+1} = \cdots$ for some $n$). This finiteness condition, named after Emmy Noether, is the single most important hypothesis in commutative algebra and algebraic geometry. Virtually all rings encountered in practice are Noetherian: fields, $\mathbb{Z}$, polynomial rings over Noetherian rings (Hilbert's basis theorem), local rings, quotient rings. The Noetherian hypothesis ensures that ideals are finitely generated, modules have finite resolutions, and algebraic varieties have finitely many irreducible components.

## Equivalent Conditions

For a commutative ring $R$, the following are equivalent:
1. **ACC**: every ascending chain of ideals stabilises.
2. **Finite generation**: every ideal of $R$ is finitely generated.
3. **Maximum condition**: every non-empty set of ideals has a maximal element.

## Examples and Non-Examples

| Ring | Noetherian? |
|---|---|
| Field $k$ | Yes |
| $\mathbb{Z}$ | Yes (PIDs are Noetherian) |
| $k[x_1,\ldots,x_n]$ | Yes (Hilbert's basis theorem) |
| $k[x_1, x_2, \ldots]$ (infinitely many variables) | No |
| $\mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{-5}]$ | Yes |

## Noetherian Modules

An $R$-module $M$ is **Noetherian** if every submodule is finitely generated, equivalently if chains of submodules stabilise. If $R$ is Noetherian, every finitely generated $R$-module is Noetherian.

## Structure Theorems

- Every ideal in a Noetherian ring is an intersection of finitely many **primary ideals** (Lasker–Noether theorem).
- The set of minimal primes over an ideal is finite.
- Noetherian rings have finite **Krull dimension** if they are also artinian or finitely generated over a field/PID.

## Glossary

| Term / Symbol | Meaning |
|---|---|
| ACC | ascending chain condition: chains of ideals stabilise |
| Noetherian ring | satisfies ACC on ideals |
| Finitely generated ideal | $I = (a_1,\ldots,a_n) = a_1R + \cdots + a_nR$ |
| Noetherian module | every submodule is finitely generated |
| Primary ideal | ideal $Q$ where $ab \in Q \Rightarrow a \in Q$ or $b^n \in Q$ |
| Lasker–Noether theorem | ideals in Noetherian rings decompose into primary ideals |
| Krull dimension | supremum of lengths of chains of prime ideals |
| PID | principal ideal domain: every ideal generated by one element |
| Artinian ring | satisfies DCC on ideals; if Noetherian + Artinian, then semisimple |
