# Integral Extensions

An element $\alpha$ of a ring extension $R \subseteq S$ is **integral** over $R$ if it satisfies a monic polynomial with coefficients in $R$: $\alpha^n + r_{n-1}\alpha^{n-1} + \cdots + r_0 = 0$ with $r_i \in R$. This generalises the notion of algebraic integer (elements of $\mathbb{C}$ integral over $\mathbb{Z}$) and is the foundation of algebraic number theory and the normalisation of algebraic varieties. The **integral closure** of $R$ in $S$ is the set of all elements of $S$ integral over $R$, which is itself a ring. Integral extensions control how primes split or ramify as one passes from $R$ to $S$, a key theme in both number theory and algebraic geometry.

## Definitions

Let $R \subseteq S$ be commutative rings. $s \in S$ is **integral over $R$** if there exists a monic polynomial:
$$s^n + r_{n-1}s^{n-1} + \cdots + r_1 s + r_0 = 0, \quad r_i \in R$$

The **integral closure** of $R$ in $S$: $\overline{R}^S = \{s \in S : s \text{ integral over } R\}$.

$R$ is **integrally closed in $S$** if $\overline{R}^S = R$. $R$ is **normal** if it is integrally closed in $\mathrm{Frac}(R)$.

## Equivalent Criterion

$s$ is integral over $R$ $\iff$ $R[s]$ is a finitely generated $R$-module $\iff$ there exists a faithful $R[s]$-module that is finitely generated as an $R$-module.

## Going-Up & Going-Down

Let $R \subseteq S$ be integral. Then:
- **Going-Up**: if $\mathfrak{p} \subseteq \mathfrak{q}$ are primes in $R$ and $\mathfrak{P}$ lies over $\mathfrak{p}$, there exists $\mathfrak{Q}$ over $\mathfrak{q}$ with $\mathfrak{P} \subseteq \mathfrak{Q}$.
- **Going-Down** (when $R$ is normal): the analogous statement descending the chain.

## Glossary

| Term / Symbol | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Integral element | satisfies a monic polynomial over $R$ |
| Monic polynomial | leading coefficient is 1 |
| Integral closure $\overline{R}^S$ | all elements of $S$ integral over $R$ |
| Normal ring | integrally closed in its field of fractions |
| Integrally closed | $\overline{R}^S = R$ |
| $R[s]$ | $R$-subalgebra generated by $s$ |
| Going-Up theorem | primes lift along integral extensions |
| Going-Down theorem | primes descend for normal base |
| Algebraic integer | element integral over $\mathbb{Z}$ |
| Normalisation | integral closure of a domain in its fraction field |
