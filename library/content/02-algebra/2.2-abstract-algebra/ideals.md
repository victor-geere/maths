## Definition

A subset $I \subseteq R$ is a **left ideal** if:
1. $(I, +)$ is a subgroup of $(R, +)$
2. $r \in R, a \in I \Rightarrow ra \in I$

A **two-sided ideal** satisfies both $ra \in I$ and $ar \in I$ for all $r \in R$, $a \in I$.

## Quotient Ring

If $I \trianglelefteq R$ (two-sided), the quotient $R/I$ is a ring with:
$$(a + I) + (b + I) = (a+b) + I, \qquad (a+I)(b+I) = ab + I$$

## Types of Ideals

- **Principal ideal:** generated by a single element: $(a) = \{ra : r \in R\}$.
- **Prime ideal:** $I$ is prime if $ab \in I \Rightarrow a \in I$ or $b \in I$. Equivalently, $R/I$ is an integral domain.
- **Maximal ideal:** no ideal $J$ with $I \subsetneq J \subsetneq R$. Equivalently, $R/I$ is a field.

## First Isomorphism Theorem (Rings)

For a ring homomorphism $\phi : R \to S$:

$$R/\ker(\phi) \cong \text{Im}(\phi)$$

## Example

$I = (2) \subseteq \mathbb{Z}$ is a prime ideal. $\mathbb{Z}/(2) \cong \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$ is a field, so $(2)$ is also maximal.

In $\mathbb{Z}[x]$, the ideal $(x^2 + 1)$ is maximal, and $\mathbb{R}[x]/(x^2+1) \cong \mathbb{C}$.
