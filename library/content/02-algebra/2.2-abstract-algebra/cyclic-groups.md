## Definition

A group $G$ is **cyclic** if there exists $g \in G$ such that every element of $G$ is a power of $g$:

$$G = \langle g \rangle = \{g^n : n \in \mathbb{Z}\}$$

The element $g$ is called a **generator** of $G$.

## Classification

Every cyclic group is isomorphic to exactly one of:
- $\mathbb{Z}$ (infinite cyclic group)
- $\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$ for some $n \geq 1$ (finite cyclic group of order $n$)

## Subgroups of Cyclic Groups

- Every subgroup of a cyclic group is cyclic.
- For $G = \mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$: for each divisor $d \mid n$, there is a **unique** subgroup of order $d$.

## Generators of $\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$

$k$ generates $\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$ iff $\gcd(k, n) = 1$.

The number of generators equals Euler's totient $\phi(n)$.

## Example

$\mathbb{Z}/6\mathbb{Z} = \{0,1,2,3,4,5\}$:

- Generators: $1$ and $5$ (since $\gcd(1,6) = \gcd(5,6) = 1$).
- Subgroups: $\{0\}$, $\{0,2,4\}$, $\{0,3\}$, $\mathbb{Z}/6\mathbb{Z}$ (orders 1, 3, 2, 6).

## Notes

- Cyclic groups are always abelian.
- A finite group of prime order is necessarily cyclic.
- The multiplicative group $(\mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z})^*$ is cyclic of order $p-1$ for any prime $p$.
