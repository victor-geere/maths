# Angles & Parallel Lines

When two parallel lines are cut by a third line (a **transversal**), the angles formed at each intersection are related by fixed rules. These relationships are foundational to Euclidean geometry and underlie the proofs of almost every theorem about triangles and polygons.

## Types of Angles

An **angle** is formed by two rays sharing a common endpoint (the **vertex**). Angles are measured in **degrees** (°) or **radians** (rad), where $360° = 2\pi$ rad.

| Name | Measure |
|---|---|
| Acute | $0° < \theta < 90°$ |
| Right | $\theta = 90°$ |
| Obtuse | $90° < \theta < 180°$ |
| Straight | $\theta = 180°$ |
| Reflex | $180° < \theta < 360°$ |

## Angle Pairs

- **Complementary:** two angles summing to $90°$.
- **Supplementary:** two angles summing to $180°$.
- **Vertical (opposite):** formed by two intersecting lines; always equal.

## Parallel Lines Cut by a Transversal

Let $\ell_1 \parallel \ell_2$ and $t$ be a transversal. The eight angles formed obey:

| Pair | Relationship |
|---|---|
| Corresponding angles | Equal ($\angle 1 = \angle 5$, etc.) |
| Alternate interior angles | Equal ($\angle 3 = \angle 6$, etc.) |
| Alternate exterior angles | Equal ($\angle 1 = \angle 8$, etc.) |
| Co-interior (same-side interior) | Supplementary ($\angle 3 + \angle 5 = 180°$) |

These are **equivalent** to the parallel postulate (Euclid's fifth postulate).

## Euclid's Parallel Postulate

If a transversal meets two lines such that the co-interior angles sum to less than $180°$, the two lines meet on that side. Equivalent forms: "through a point not on a line, there is exactly one parallel line" (Playfair's axiom).

## Example

Lines $\ell_1 \parallel \ell_2$ are cut by transversal $t$. One co-interior angle is $65°$. Find its partner.

$$65° + \theta = 180° \implies \theta = 115°$$

## Glossary

| Term / Symbol | Meaning |
|---|---|
| $\angle$ | angle |
| $\parallel$ | parallel to |
| $\perp$ | perpendicular to |
| $°$ | degrees |
| rad | radians ($2\pi$ rad $= 360°$) |
| Transversal | a line crossing two or more other lines |
| Vertex | the common endpoint of two rays forming an angle |
| Corresponding angles | angles in the same position at each intersection |
| Alternate interior angles | angles between the parallels on opposite sides of the transversal |
| Co-interior angles | angles between the parallels on the same side of the transversal |
| Supplementary | two angles summing to $180°$ |
| Complementary | two angles summing to $90°$ |
