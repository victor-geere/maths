# Triangles & Congruence

A **triangle** is the simplest polygon — three vertices, three sides, three interior angles — and it is the building block of all planar geometry. **Congruence** is the precise notion that two triangles are identical in shape and size: one can be placed exactly on top of the other by rigid motions (translations, rotations, reflections). Congruence criteria give us minimal conditions to confirm this without measuring all six parts.

## Angle Sum

The three interior angles of any triangle sum to $180°$:

$$\alpha + \beta + \gamma = 180°$$

## Types of Triangle

| By sides | By angles |
|---|---|
| Equilateral ($a = b = c$) | Acute (all angles $< 90°$) |
| Isosceles ($a = b$) | Right (one angle $= 90°$) |
| Scalene (all different) | Obtuse (one angle $> 90°$) |

## Congruence Criteria (SSS, SAS, ASA, AAS, RHS)

Two triangles $\triangle ABC \cong \triangle DEF$ if any of the following hold:

| Criterion | Conditions |
|---|---|
| **SSS** | $a=d,\; b=e,\; c=f$ (all three sides) |
| **SAS** | Two sides and the **included** angle equal |
| **ASA** | Two angles and the **included** side equal |
| **AAS** | Two angles and a **non-included** side equal |
| **RHS** | Right angle, hypotenuse, one side (right triangles only) |

Note: SSA is **not** a valid congruence criterion in general (ambiguous case).

## Key Theorems

### Isosceles Triangle Theorem
If $AB = AC$, then $\angle B = \angle C$ (base angles are equal).

### Exterior Angle Theorem
An exterior angle of a triangle equals the sum of the two non-adjacent interior angles.

$$\angle_{ext} = \alpha + \beta$$

### Triangle Inequality
$$|a - b| < c < a + b$$

Any side length must lie strictly between the absolute difference and the sum of the other two.

## Pythagoras' Theorem (Right Triangles)

$$a^2 + b^2 = c^2$$

where $c$ is the hypotenuse (side opposite the right angle).

## Glossary

| Term / Symbol | Meaning |
|---|---|
| $\triangle ABC$ | triangle with vertices $A$, $B$, $C$ |
| $\cong$ | congruent (identical shape and size) |
| $a, b, c$ | side lengths (often $a$ opposite $\angle A$, etc.) |
| $\alpha, \beta, \gamma$ | interior angles at vertices $A$, $B$, $C$ |
| Vertex (pl. vertices) | a corner point of the triangle |
| Hypotenuse | the side opposite the right angle in a right triangle |
| Equilateral | all three sides equal |
| Isosceles | exactly two sides equal |
| Scalene | all three sides different |
| SSS / SAS / ASA / AAS / RHS | congruence criteria (Side-Side-Side, etc.) |
| Exterior angle | angle formed outside the triangle at a vertex |
| Triangle inequality | $|a-b| < c < a+b$ for any triangle |
