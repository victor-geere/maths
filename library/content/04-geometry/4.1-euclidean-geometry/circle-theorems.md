# Circle Theorems

A **circle** is the set of all points in a plane at a fixed distance (the **radius**) from a fixed point (the **centre**). The classical circle theorems describe how angles subtended by chords, the relationship between tangents and radii, and the properties of cyclic quadrilaterals all emerge from the single constraint of equidistance. These theorems are among the oldest results in geometry and remain essential in modern mathematics.

## Fundamental Definitions

- **Radius** $r$: distance from centre $O$ to any point on the circle.
- **Diameter** $d = 2r$: a chord passing through the centre.
- **Chord**: a line segment joining two points on the circle.
- **Arc**: a portion of the circle's circumference.
- **Tangent**: a line touching the circle at exactly one point.
- **Secant**: a line intersecting the circle at two points.

## Key Theorems

### 1. Angle at the Centre
The angle subtended at the centre by an arc is **twice** the angle subtended at any point on the remaining arc:

$$\angle AOB = 2\, \angle ACB$$

### 2. Angles in the Same Segment
All angles subtended by the same chord from the same side of it are **equal**:

$$\angle ACB = \angle ADB \quad (C, D \text{ on same arc})$$

### 3. Angle in a Semicircle (Thales)
The angle subtended by a diameter at the circumference is always $90°$:

$$AB \text{ is diameter} \implies \angle ACB = 90°$$

### 4. Cyclic Quadrilateral
Opposite angles of a cyclic quadrilateral (all four vertices on the circle) sum to $180°$:

$$\angle A + \angle C = 180°, \qquad \angle B + \angle D = 180°$$

### 5. Tangent–Radius
A tangent to a circle is **perpendicular** to the radius at the point of contact:

$$OT \perp \text{tangent at } T$$

### 6. Tangent from an External Point
Two tangents drawn from an external point to a circle have **equal length**:

$$PA = PB \quad (P \text{ external, } A,B \text{ points of tangency})$$

### 7. Alternate Segment (Tangent-Chord Angle)
The angle between a tangent and a chord equals the inscribed angle on the opposite side:

$$\angle(\text{tangent}, AB) = \angle ACB \quad (C \text{ in alternate segment})$$

## Circumference and Area

$$C = 2\pi r, \qquad A = \pi r^2$$

## Glossary

| Term / Symbol | Meaning |
|---|---|
| $r$ | radius of the circle |
| $d$ | diameter ($d = 2r$) |
| $O$ | centre of the circle |
| $\pi$ | pi ($\approx 3.14159$) |
| Chord | line segment joining two points on the circle |
| Arc | part of the circumference |
| Tangent | line touching the circle at exactly one point |
| Secant | line cutting through the circle at two points |
| Inscribed angle | angle formed by two chords with vertex on the circle |
| Central angle | angle at the centre subtended by an arc |
| Cyclic quadrilateral | quadrilateral with all four vertices on a circle |
| Alternate segment | the region of the circle on the opposite side of a chord from a given point |
| Subtend | an arc or chord "subtends" an angle if its endpoints are the two rays of that angle |
