# σ-Algebras & Measurable Spaces

A **σ-algebra** (sigma-algebra) on a set $X$ is a collection $\mathcal{F}$ of subsets of $X$ that is closed under countable unions, countable intersections, and complements, and contains $X$ itself. The pair $(X, \mathcal{F})$ is called a **measurable space**, and the elements of $\mathcal{F}$ are called **measurable sets**. σ-algebras are the domains of measures: a measure assigns a non-negative real number to each measurable set. The choice of σ-algebra determines what can be measured; the **Borel σ-algebra** on $\mathbb{R}$ (generated by open sets) and the **Lebesgue σ-algebra** (its completion) are the most important in analysis and probability. Measurability is also the correct notion for functions between measurable spaces: $f: (X, \mathcal{F}) \to (Y, \mathcal{G})$ is measurable iff $f^{-1}(B) \in \mathcal{F}$ for all $B \in \mathcal{G}$.

## Definition

A **σ-algebra** on $X$ is a collection $\mathcal{F} \subseteq 2^X$ satisfying:
1. $X \in \mathcal{F}$
2. $A \in \mathcal{F} \Rightarrow A^c \in \mathcal{F}$ (closed under complements)
3. $A_1, A_2, \ldots \in \mathcal{F} \Rightarrow \bigcup_{n=1}^\infty A_n \in \mathcal{F}$ (closed under countable unions)

## Generated σ-Algebra

For any collection $\mathcal{C} \subseteq 2^X$, the **σ-algebra generated by $\mathcal{C}$** is:
$$\sigma(\mathcal{C}) = \bigcap\{\mathcal{F} : \mathcal{F}\text{ is a }\sigma\text{-algebra}, \mathcal{C} \subseteq \mathcal{F}\}$$
the smallest σ-algebra containing $\mathcal{C}$.

## Borel σ-Algebra

$\mathcal{B}(\mathbb{R}) = \sigma(\{\text{open sets}\})$: generated by open sets of $\mathbb{R}$. Contains all open, closed, $G_\delta$, $F_\sigma$ sets and much more.

## Measurable Functions

$f: (X,\mathcal{F}) \to (Y,\mathcal{G})$ is **measurable** if $f^{-1}(B) \in \mathcal{F}$ for all $B \in \mathcal{G}$.

## Glossary

| Term / Symbol | Meaning |
|---|---|
| $\mathcal{F}$ | σ-algebra on $X$ |
| $(X, \mathcal{F})$ | measurable space |
| Measurable set | element of $\mathcal{F}$ |
| $\sigma(\mathcal{C})$ | σ-algebra generated by $\mathcal{C}$ |
| $\mathcal{B}(\mathbb{R})$ | Borel σ-algebra: $\sigma($open sets$)$ |
| $A^c = X \setminus A$ | complement of $A$ |
| Countable union | $\bigcup_{n=1}^\infty A_n$ |
| Measurable function | $f^{-1}(B) \in \mathcal{F}$ for all $B \in \mathcal{G}$ |
| $G_\delta$ set | countable intersection of open sets |
| $F_\sigma$ set | countable union of closed sets |
