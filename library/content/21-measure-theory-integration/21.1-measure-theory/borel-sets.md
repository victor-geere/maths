# Borel Sets

The **Borel σ-algebra** $\mathcal{B}(X)$ on a topological space $X$ is the σ-algebra generated by the open sets: $\mathcal{B}(X) = \sigma(\{U \subseteq X : U \text{ open}\})$. Its members are called **Borel sets**. Every open set, every closed set, every countable intersection of open sets ($G_\delta$), and every countable union of closed sets ($F_\sigma$) is a Borel set. On $\mathbb{R}$, the Borel sets are generated equivalently by open intervals, closed intervals, or half-open intervals $(a,b]$. The Borel σ-algebra is the natural domain for measures that interact well with the topology (Borel measures, Radon measures). The Lebesgue σ-algebra is strictly larger than $\mathcal{B}(\mathbb{R})$ — it includes all subsets of Lebesgue null sets — and Borel sets are always Lebesgue measurable.

## Borel Hierarchy

Starting from open sets $\Sigma_1^0$ and closed sets $\Pi_1^0$:
- $G_\delta$ ($\Pi_2^0$): countable intersections of open sets (e.g., $\mathbb{Q}^c \cap [0,1]$)
- $F_\sigma$ ($\Sigma_2^0$): countable unions of closed sets (e.g., $\mathbb{Q}$)
- $G_{\delta\sigma}$: countable unions of $G_\delta$ sets
- $F_{\sigma\delta}$: countable intersections of $F_\sigma$ sets
- Continuing transfinitely through $\omega_1$ many levels...

All levels together form $\mathcal{B}(\mathbb{R})$.

## Borel Functions

$f: X \to Y$ is **Borel measurable** (or Borel) if $f^{-1}(U) \in \mathcal{B}(X)$ for all open $U \subseteq Y$. Every continuous function is Borel; every monotone function $\mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}$ is Borel.

## Glossary

| Term / Symbol | Meaning |
|---|---|
| $\mathcal{B}(X)$ | Borel σ-algebra: $\sigma($open sets of $X)$ |
| Borel set | member of $\mathcal{B}(X)$ |
| $G_\delta$ | countable intersection of open sets |
| $F_\sigma$ | countable union of closed sets |
| $G_{\delta\sigma}$ | countable union of $G_\delta$ sets |
| Borel hierarchy | $\Sigma_n^0, \Pi_n^0, \Delta_n^0$; transfinite stratification |
| Borel measure | measure defined on $\mathcal{B}(X)$ |
| Borel measurable function | $f^{-1}($open$) \in \mathcal{B}$ |
| Analytic set | continuous image of Borel set; may not be Borel |
| Lebesgue vs Borel | $\mathcal{B}(\mathbb{R}) \subsetneq \mathcal{L}(\mathbb{R})$; all Borel sets are Lebesgue measurable |
