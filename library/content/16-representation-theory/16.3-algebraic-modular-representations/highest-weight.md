# Highest-Weight Theory

**Highest-weight theory** is the complete classification of irreducible finite-dimensional representations of semisimple (and reductive) Lie algebras. Every such representation has a **highest weight**: a vector annihilated by all positive root operators, on which the Cartan acts by a **dominant integral** linear functional $\lambda \in \mathfrak{h}^*$. Conversely, for every dominant integral weight $\lambda$, there exists a unique irreducible module $L(\lambda)$. This reduces the problem of classifying representations to the combinatorics of the weight lattice — a beautiful interplay between algebra and geometry. The theory extends naturally to algebraic groups, quantum groups, and Kac–Moody algebras.

## Setup

Let $\mathfrak{g}$ be a complex semisimple Lie algebra with Cartan subalgebra $\mathfrak{h}$, root system $\Phi = \Phi^+ \cup \Phi^-$, and simple roots $\alpha_1,\ldots,\alpha_r$.

The **weight lattice** is $P = \{\lambda \in \mathfrak{h}^* : \frac{2\langle\lambda,\alpha_i\rangle}{\langle\alpha_i,\alpha_i\rangle} \in \mathbb{Z}\ \forall i\}$.

**Dominant integral weights**: $P^+ = \{\lambda \in P : \langle\lambda,\alpha_i^\vee\rangle \geq 0\ \forall i\}$, where $\alpha_i^\vee = \frac{2\alpha_i}{\langle\alpha_i,\alpha_i\rangle}$.

## Highest Weight Modules

A **highest weight vector** $v_\lambda$ of weight $\lambda$ satisfies:
$$h \cdot v_\lambda = \lambda(h)v_\lambda \quad \forall h \in \mathfrak{h}, \qquad e_\alpha \cdot v_\lambda = 0 \quad \forall \alpha \in \Phi^+$$

The **Verma module** $M(\lambda) = U(\mathfrak{g}) \otimes_{U(\mathfrak{b})} k_\lambda$ is the universal highest-weight module. It has a unique maximal submodule, and the quotient is the **irreducible module** $L(\lambda)$.

## Classification Theorem

**Theorem**: The map $\lambda \mapsto L(\lambda)$ is a bijection from $P^+$ to the set of isomorphism classes of irreducible finite-dimensional $\mathfrak{g}$-modules.

## Weyl Character Formula

The character of $L(\lambda)$ is:
$$\chi_{L(\lambda)} = \frac{\sum_{w \in W} (-1)^{\ell(w)} e^{w(\lambda+\rho)-\rho}}{\prod_{\alpha \in \Phi^+}(1 - e^{-\alpha})}$$
where $W$ is the Weyl group, $\rho = \frac{1}{2}\sum_{\alpha \in \Phi^+} \alpha$, and $\ell(w)$ is the length of $w$.

## Glossary

| Term / Symbol | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Dominant integral weight $\lambda \in P^+$ | $\langle\lambda,\alpha_i^\vee\rangle \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}$ for all simple roots |
| Weight lattice $P$ | lattice in $\mathfrak{h}^*$ of integral linear functionals |
| $\alpha_i^\vee = 2\alpha_i/|\alpha_i|^2$ | coroot of simple root $\alpha_i$ |
| $\Phi^+, \Phi^-$ | positive and negative roots |
| Highest weight vector $v_\lambda$ | killed by all $e_\alpha$, $\alpha > 0$ |
| Verma module $M(\lambda)$ | universal highest weight module |
| Irreducible module $L(\lambda)$ | unique quotient of $M(\lambda)$ |
| $U(\mathfrak{g})$ | universal enveloping algebra |
| Weyl group $W$ | group generated by reflections in root system |
| $\rho$ | half-sum of positive roots (Weyl vector) |
| Weyl character formula | formula for $\chi_{L(\lambda)}$ in terms of $W$ and $\rho$ |
