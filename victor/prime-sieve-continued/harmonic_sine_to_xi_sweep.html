1. Introduction & Overview

This document synthesizes a complete journey: starting from the harmonic (greedy Egyptian/unit-fraction) reconstruction of the sine function for rational angles (finite exact products of rotations), through its analytic continuation to the classical Euler–Weierstrass product on the complex plane, its logarithmic encoding of the even Riemann zeta values, and finally the structural analogy that allows us to sweep the zero set one pair at a time from the integers to the actual non-trivial zeros of the Riemann zeta function — all without assuming the Riemann hypothesis.

The key unifying idea is the Weierstrass/Hadamard canonical product: entire functions of finite order are built from their zeros. The sine function is the canonical example with zeros at the integers. The completed Riemann xi function \(\xi(s)\) is the analogous object whose zeros are precisely the non-trivial zeros of \(\zeta(s)\).

Core analogy
\[ \frac{\sin(\pi t)}{\pi t} = \prod_{n=1}^\infty \left(1 - \frac{t^2}{n^2}\right) \qquad \longleftrightarrow \qquad \Xi(t) = \Xi(0) \prod_{k=1}^\infty \left(1 - \frac{t^2}{t_k^2}\right) \] where \( t_k = \operatorname{Im}(\rho_k) \) are the imaginary parts of the non-trivial zeros \(\rho_k\) of \(\zeta(s)\).

2. Finite Harmonic Reconstruction for Rational Angles

For rational \( t = a/b \in [0,1) \), the greedy Egyptian-fraction (Sylvester–Fibonacci) expansion \( t = \sum_{k=1}^K 1/q_k \) (with \( q_k \) strictly increasing integers \(\ge 2\)) terminates after finitely many steps (\( K \le a \)).

Closed form (from the harmonic reconstruction)
\[ \sin(\pi t) = \operatorname{Im} \prod_{k=1}^K \exp\left( i \frac{\pi}{q_k} \right) \]

Explicit expansion with sine, cosine, and symbolic \( i^2 \)

We compute the product by successive complex multiplication, keeping the imaginary unit \( i \) and the symbol \( i^2 \) explicit until the end, then apply the angle-addition identity.

Worked example: \( t = 3/4 = 1/2 + 1/4 \) (\( q_1 = 2 \), \( q_2 = 4 \))

Factor 1: \(\exp(i \pi/2) = 0 + i \cdot 1 = i\)
Factor 2: \(\exp(i \pi/4) = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} + i \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\)

Product (keeping \( i^2 \) symbolic):
\[ i \cdot \left( \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} + i \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \right) = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} i + i^2 \cdot \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \]

Apply addition identity to accumulated angle \(\pi/2 + \pi/4 = 3\pi/4\):
\[ \sin\left(\frac{\pi}{2} + \frac{\pi}{4}\right) = \sin\frac{\pi}{2}\cos\frac{\pi}{4} + \cos\frac{\pi}{2}\sin\frac{\pi}{4} = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \]
After substituting the value of the symbol \( i^2 = -1 \), the imaginary part of the product is exactly \(\sqrt{2}/2 = \sin(3\pi/4)\).

The same recursive process (or expanded odd-subset sum of the imaginary contributions) works for any finite greedy expansion. The real part of the product is always the cosine of the accumulated angle, carried forward exactly.

3. Analytic Continuation to \(\mathbb{C}\) — The Euler Product

When \( t \) is irrational the greedy process never terminates, but the partial products converge (doubly exponentially). The limit yields the globally valid expression on the complex plane:

\[ \sin(\pi z) = \pi z \prod_{n=1}^{\infty} \left(1 - \frac{z^2}{n^2}\right), \qquad z \in \mathbb{C}. \] This is the Weierstrass canonical product over the zeros \(\pm n\) of sine. It is already the analytic continuation of the finite harmonic construction.

4. Logarithmic Expansion & Relation to Riemann Zeta & Dirichlet Eta

Taking the logarithm of the normalized product (for \( |z| < 1 \)) and expanding:

\[ \log\left( \frac{\sin(\pi z)}{\pi z} \right) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \log\left(1 - \frac{z^2}{n^2}\right) = -\sum_{m=1}^{\infty} \frac{\zeta(2m)}{m} z^{2m}. \] Thus \[ \sin(\pi z) = \pi z \exp\left( -\sum_{m=1}^{\infty} \frac{\zeta(2m)}{m} z^{2m} \right). \]

The coefficients are exactly the even positive integer values of the Riemann zeta function.

Connection to Dirichlet eta function \(\eta(s)\): The eta function \(\eta(s) = (1-2^{1-s})\zeta(s)\) converges for \(\operatorname{Re}(s) > 0\) and is the natural tool for proving the functional equation of zeta. The Gamma reflection formula \[ \Gamma(z)\Gamma(1-z) = \frac{\pi}{\sin(\pi z)} \] directly inserts our sine product. The completed xi function that encodes the non-trivial zeros is built from this Gamma factor together with \(\zeta(s)\).

6. The Paired Product for \(\Xi(t)\) — Direct Analogue

Define the Riemann–Siegel Xi function \(\Xi(t) := \xi(\frac12 + i t)\). It is even and real-valued for real \( t \). Its zeros are exactly \(\pm t_k\).

\[ \Xi(t) = \Xi(0) \prod_{k=1}^{\infty} \left(1 - \frac{t^2}{t_k^2}\right). \] This is the precise structural analogue of the sine product, with the integers \( n \) replaced by the ordinates \( t_k \) of the non-trivial zeros.

7. Convergence Factors in the Hadamard Product

Both sine and \(\xi(s)\) are entire of order 1. The general Hadamard factorization therefore includes genus-1 exponential convergence factors:

\[ \xi(s) = \xi(0) \prod_{\rho} \left(1 - \frac{s}{\rho}\right) \exp\left( \frac{s}{\rho} \right), \] where the product runs over all non-trivial zeros \(\rho\). The factor \(\exp(s/\rho)\) guarantees convergence.

When zeros are paired as \(\rho_n = \frac12 + i t_n\) and its conjugate (or the functional-equation quartet), the linear exponential terms cancel in the paired quadratic factors, recovering the clean product for \(\Xi(t)\) with no extra exponential factors.

8. Explicit Formula: Primes from Summing over \( t_n \)

The von Mangoldt explicit formula expresses the Chebyshev function in terms of the non-trivial zeros:

\[ \psi_0(x) = x - \sum_{\rho} \frac{x^\rho}{\rho} - \log(2\pi) - \frac12 \log(1 - x^{-2}), \] (symmetric limit over zeros). Substituting \(\rho = \frac12 + i t_n\) (and conjugates) yields an explicit oscillatory sum over the frequencies \( t_n \): \[ -x^{1/2} \sum_n \left[ \frac{x^{i t_n}}{\frac12 + i t_n} + \text{c.c.} \right]. \] This is how the imaginary parts \( t_n \) “recover the primes”.

9. Sweeping One Zero at a Time (Deformation on \(\mathbb{C}\), No RH Assumed)

We now perform the central construction of this document: a continuous deformation that sweeps the zero set from the integers (sine) to the actual non-trivial zeros of \(\zeta(s)\), one pair at a time, using only complex analysis and the Weierstrass theorem. No assumption is made that any zero lies on the critical line.

Stage \(\lambda = 0\): Zero set = \(\{\pm n \mid n \in \mathbb{N}\}\). The canonical product recovers (a multiple of) \(\sin(\pi z)\).
Continuous paths: Enumerate the actual non-trivial zeros \(\rho_j\) (with functional-equation symmetries). Choose continuous paths \(\gamma_j(\lambda)\) in \(\mathbb{C}\) for \(\lambda \in [0,1]\) such that \(\gamma_j(0)\) is the corresponding integer and \(\gamma_j(1) = \rho_j\) (and similarly for symmetric partners). Paths avoid collisions and the origin.
One zero (pair) at a time: At stage \( N \), form the partial canonical Weierstrass product \[ P_N(z; \lambda) = z^m \exp(Q_N(z;\lambda)) \prod_{j=1}^N \left(1 - \frac{z}{\gamma_j(\lambda)}\right) \exp\left( \frac{z}{\gamma_j(\lambda)} \right). \] Each new factor introduces (or moves) exactly one simple zero while preserving analyticity. The exponential polynomial \( Q_N \) enforces convergence and symmetry at that finite stage.
Limit \(\lambda \to 1\): The infinite product converges uniformly on compact sets and defines an entire function of order 1 whose zeros are exactly the actual non-trivial zeros \(\{\rho_j\}\). This function coincides with the completed xi function \(\xi(z)\) up to a multiplicative factor \(\exp(a + b z)\) fixed by the functional equation and the value at zero.

At every intermediate \(\lambda\) the product is an entire function whose zeros are the current positions \(\gamma_j(\lambda)\). When a zero reaches its final location \(\rho_j\), the corresponding factor forces a zero of the limiting function exactly there — this is the “analytic continued non-trivial zero” produced by the sweep.

12. Conclusion & Implications

The harmonic unit-fraction reconstruction provides an exact finite closed form for rational angles via finite products of rotations. Its analytic continuation yields the Euler product whose logarithm directly encodes the even zeta values. The identical Weierstrass product logic, when applied to the zero set of \(\xi(s)\), replaces the integer labels by the actual non-trivial zeros \(\rho_j\) (wherever they lie). The continuous deformation described in Section 9 performs this replacement one zero (pair) at a time on the complex plane, using only the synthetic imaginary unit and the canonical product construction — without any assumption that the zeros lie on the critical line.

This establishes a rigorous structural bridge between the elementary harmonic sine and the deepest object in analytic number theory: the Riemann xi function whose zeros encode the primes.
