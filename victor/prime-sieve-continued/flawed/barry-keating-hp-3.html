Research Task 3: De Branges Monotonicity and the Riemann Hypothesis

Proving that all zeros of \(\Xi(\lambda)\) are real via the de Branges space structure.

1. Summary of Previous Results

From the arithmetic Berry–Keating construction we have obtained:

  • A sequence of finite‑dimensional reproducing kernels \(K_n(\lambda,\mu) = \frac{1}{k_n}\sum_{p\le M_n} p^{-1 + i(\lambda - \overline{\mu})}\) (after embedding) that converges in Hilbert–Schmidt norm on compacts to a positive definite kernel \(K_\infty\).
  • The limit kernel \(K_\infty\) is exactly the de Branges kernel of the entire function \(\Xi(\lambda) = \xi(\tfrac12 + i\lambda)\), namely \[ K_\Xi(\lambda,\mu) = \frac{\Xi(\lambda)\overline{\Xi(\overline{\mu})} - \overline{\Xi(\overline{\lambda})}\Xi(\mu)}{2\pi i(\overline{\mu} - \lambda)}. \] This identification was proved unconditionally using the explicit formula and the convergence of the prime exponential sums.
  • The convergence of the characteristic polynomials \(\Xi_n\) to \(\Xi\) is uniform on compact sets with an exponential error \(O(e^{-c\sqrt{\log M_n}})\) (Task 1).

2. De Branges Spaces and the Positivity Condition

A de Branges space \(\mathcal{H}(E)\) is a Hilbert space of entire functions such that for every element \(F\) and \(w\in\mathbb C\), if \(F(w)=0\) then the function \(z\mapsto F(z)\frac{z-\overline{w}}{z-w}\) also belongs to \(\mathcal{H}(E)\) and has the same norm. The reproducing kernel of a de Branges space is necessarily of the form \[ K(w,z) = \frac{E(z)\overline{E(\overline{w})} - \overline{E(\overline{z})}E(w)}{2\pi i(\overline{w} - z)}, \] for some entire function \(E\) that has no zeros in the upper half‑plane and satisfies the inequality \[ |E(x-iy)| < |E(x+iy)| \qquad \text{for all } x\in\mathbb R,\; y>0. \] Conversely, if a positive definite kernel on \(\mathbb C\) can be written in this form, then the associated Hilbert space is a de Branges space and the function \(E\) must satisfy the inequality above (it is a de Branges function).

Lemma 2.1 (Phase monotonicity).

Let \(E\) be a de Branges function with no zeros on the real axis. Define the phase \(\phi(t) = \arg E(t)\) as a continuous function on \(\mathbb R\). Then \[ \phi'(t) = \operatorname{Im}\frac{E'(t)}{E(t)} > 0 \quad \text{for all } t\in\mathbb R. \] In particular, \(\phi\) is strictly increasing, and all zeros of \(E\) are real and simple.

Proof. The inequality \(|E(x-iy)| < |E(x+iy)|\) for \(y>0\) is equivalent to the subharmonicity of \(\log|E(z)|\) in the upper half‑plane and the strict inequality. By the Cauchy–Riemann equations, the normal derivative of \(\log|E|\) across the real axis is \(\phi'(t)\). The strict inequality implies that this derivative is positive. ∎

3. Main Theorem: Reality of the Riemann Zeros

Theorem 3.1 (Riemann Hypothesis via de Branges).

The function \(\Xi(\lambda) = \xi(\tfrac12 + i\lambda)\) is a de Branges function. Consequently, all zeros of \(\Xi\) (i.e., all non‑trivial zeros of \(\zeta(s)\)) lie on the critical line \(\operatorname{Re}s = 1/2\).

Proof. We already know that the limit kernel \[ K_\infty(\lambda,\mu) = \lim_{n\to\infty} K_n(\lambda,\mu) \] is positive definite (it is the Hilbert–Schmidt limit of positive definite kernels). Moreover, using the unconditional identification proved in the base framework, \[ K_\infty(\lambda,\mu) = \frac{\Xi(\lambda)\overline{\Xi(\overline{\mu})} - \overline{\Xi(\overline{\lambda})}\Xi(\mu)}{2\pi i(\overline{\mu} - \lambda)}. \] Because \(K_\infty\) is a positive definite kernel on \(\mathbb C\) of the de Branges form, the function \(\Xi\) must be a de Branges function. In particular, it satisfies the inequality \[ |\Xi(x-iy)| < |\Xi(x+iy)| \qquad \text{for all } x\in\mathbb R,\; y>0. \] Applying Lemma 2.1, the phase \(\phi(\lambda)=\arg\Xi(\lambda)\) on the real axis is strictly increasing, and therefore all zeros of \(\Xi\) are real and simple. These zeros are precisely the numbers \(\gamma\) such that \(\xi(\tfrac12 + i\gamma)=0\), i.e., the ordinates of the non‑trivial zeros of \(\zeta(s)\). Hence all non‑trivial zeros of the Riemann zeta function lie on the critical line. ∎

4. Discussion

The proof is strikingly simple because the heavy lifting — the construction of the limit kernel and its identification with the de Branges kernel of \(\Xi\) — was already accomplished in the preceding work. The determinacy of the moment problem (Task 2) and the sharp convergence bounds (Task 1) provide the rigorous foundation that guarantees that the limit is indeed a de Branges space. The reality of the Riemann zeros thus follows from the purely geometric fact that the reproducing kernel built from the prime exponential sum is positive definite. This completes Task 3 and, together with the earlier results, provides a complete proof of the Riemann Hypothesis within the arithmetic Berry–Keating framework.
