Hermite–Biehler Positivity for Shifted \(\Xi\)
A Kernel-Positivity Criterion Equivalent to the Riemann Hypothesis

Research note — 3 July 2026

Abstract

We prove that the Riemann Hypothesis is equivalent to a Hermite–Biehler positivity statement about the vertical translates \(E_h(z) = \Xi(z+ih)\) of the Riemann \(\Xi\) function, for every \(h>0\): equivalently, to the positive semi-definiteness on the upper half-plane of the de Branges kernel attached to \(E_h\). The proof is a three-way equivalence, complete in both directions, using only the unconditional even Hadamard factorisation of \(\Xi\), a Schur-function contraction argument for the Szegő kernel, and an elementary cascade argument ruling out non-isolated zeros of a nonzero entire function. We are explicit about what this equivalence does and does not achieve: it reformulates RH in de Branges-space language, correcting a naive but false claim (that \(\Xi\) itself is a de Branges structure function, refuted in barry-keating-addendum-errata.html, Lemma L2) found in several recent Hilbert–Pólya proposals — but it does not make RH easier: the equivalence is a wall, not a bridge, and its content is close to a direct restatement of "all zeros are real" in the language of Hermite–Biehler classes. We record this honestly, distinguish the construction from the much deeper de Bruijn–Newman deformation (which is a genuine heat-flow smoothing of \(\Xi\), not a translation of its argument), and verify the criterion numerically at several points consistent with the (independently, computationally verified) location of the first zeros.

1. Introduction

A recurring strategy in the literature attempting a spectral proof of RH is to exhibit \(\Xi(\lambda) = \xi(\tfrac12+i\lambda)\) as the structure function of a de Branges space, and to derive reality of its zeros from the general theory of such spaces (Hermite–Biehler zeros are real and simple). This cannot work as stated: \(\Xi\) is a real entire function (\(\overline{\Xi(\bar\lambda)}=\Xi(\lambda)\)), and no real entire function can be a de Branges structure function, because the Hermite–Biehler inequality \(|E(x-iy)|<|E(x+iy)|\) collapses to equality for real \(E\). The correct move — well precedented in de Branges-space treatments of \(\zeta\) (Lagarias' survey; Burnol's Sonine spaces) — is to apply the theory not to \(\Xi\) itself but to a genuinely non-real perturbation of it. The simplest such perturbation is the vertical translate \(E_h(z) = \Xi(z+ih)\), \(h>0\): manifestly not real (its zeros are shifted off any fixed real correspondence unless the original zeros are real). We show this shift alone already captures RH exactly.

We state the result as a three-way equivalence — RH, the Hermite–Biehler property of every \(E_h\), and positive semi-definiteness of the associated de Branges kernel — and prove all implications in full. §5 discusses precisely how strong (or weak) a result this is.

2. Preliminaries

Hadamard factorisation. \(\Xi\) is entire of order 1, even, real on \(\mathbb R\), with \(\Xi(0)=\xi(\tfrac12)\ne0\). Its zeros form a multiset symmetric under \(\lambda\mapsto-\lambda\) and under conjugation; write them as \(\{\pm\gamma_j\}\) (with \(\gamma_j\) possibly complex, \(|\mathrm{Im}\,\gamma_j|<\tfrac12\), and \(\sum_j|\gamma_j|^{-2}<\infty\)). Pairing genus-one Hadamard factors of \(\pm\gamma_j\) and using evenness to cancel the linear exponential term (classical; Titchmarsh §2.12) gives, unconditionally, \[ \Xi(z) = \Xi(0)\prod_j\left(1-\frac{z^2}{\gamma_j^2}\right). \]

De Branges kernel. For entire \(E\) put \(E^{*}(z) := \overline{E(\bar z)}\). The (sesquilinear) de Branges kernel of \(E\) is \[ K_E(w,z) = \frac{i\big[E(z)\overline{E(w)} - E^{*}(z)\overline{E^{*}(w)}\big]}{2\pi(z-\bar w)}, \qquad K_E(z,z) = \frac{|E(z)|^2-|E^{*}(z)|^2}{4\pi\,\mathrm{Im}\,z}. \] \(E\) is Hermite–Biehler (HB) if \(|E^{*}(z)|<|E(z)|\) for all \(z\in\mathbb C^+ = \{\mathrm{Im}\,z>0\}\); in that case \(K_E\) is the reproducing kernel of the de Branges space \(\mathcal H(E)\), and \(K_E\succeq0\) on \(\mathbb C^+\times\mathbb C^+\).

Hermite–Biehler theorem (Levin, ch. VII). If \(E=A-iB\) is HB with \(A,B\) real entire, then \(A\) and \(B\) have only real, simple, interlacing zeros.

3. Statement

Theorem 3.1.

For \(h>0\) let \(E_h(z) = \Xi(z+ih)\), so \(E_h^{*}(z) = \Xi(z-ih)\), and let \(K_h\) be its de Branges kernel. The following are equivalent:

  1. (RH) All zeros of \(\Xi\) are real;
  2. \(E_h\) is Hermite–Biehler, for every \(h>0\);
  3. \(K_h \succeq 0\) on \(\mathbb C^+\times\mathbb C^+\), for every \(h>0\).

Note \((2)\iff(3)\) is close to definitional given the general de Branges theory (HB \(\Rightarrow\) positive kernel is standard; we supply the converse direction explicitly in the proof below, since it is the direction that certifies RH from a positivity statement, and is the one of actual interest). The substantial content is the equivalence with (1), proved in both directions in §4.

4. Proof

Step \((1)\Rightarrow(2)\).

If all \(\gamma_j\) are real, then \(E_h\) is Hermite–Biehler for every \(h>0\).

Proof. With all \(\gamma_j\in\mathbb R\), factor \(E_h(z) = \Xi(0)\prod_j\big(1-\tfrac{(z+ih)^2}{\gamma_j^2}\big) = \Xi(0)\prod_j \tfrac{(\gamma_j-z-ih)(\gamma_j+z+ih)}{\gamma_j^2}\), and correspondingly \(E_h^{*}(z) = \Xi(0)\prod_j\tfrac{(\gamma_j-z+ih)(\gamma_j+z-ih)}{\gamma_j^2}\). For \(z=x+iy\), \(y>0\), and each real \(\gamma\): \[ |\gamma - z - ih|^2 = (\gamma-x)^2+(y+h)^2, \qquad |\gamma-z+ih|^2 = (\gamma-x)^2+(h-y)^2, \] and since \(y,h>0\), \((h-y)^2 < (h+y)^2\), so \(|\gamma-z+ih| < |\gamma-z-ih|\); symmetrically \(|\gamma+z-ih|<|\gamma+z+ih|\). Hence each factor of \(E_h^{*}(z)/E_h(z)\) has modulus \(<1\). The infinite product converges absolutely (factors \(=1+O(\gamma_j^{-2})\) uniformly on compacts, by \(\sum|\gamma_j|^{-2}<\infty\)), so the full ratio has modulus strictly less than 1 on \(\mathbb C^+\): \(E_h\) is HB. \(\blacksquare\)

Step \((2)\Rightarrow(3)\).

If \(E_h\) is HB, then \(K_h\succeq0\) on \(\mathbb C^+\times\mathbb C^+\).

Proof. Let \(\varphi = E_h^{*}/E_h\), holomorphic on \(\mathbb C^+\) (no poles there, since \(E_h\)'s zeros — being \(\gamma_j - ih\) for zeros \(\gamma_j\) of \(\Xi\) with \(\mathrm{Im}\,\gamma_j<\ldots\) — need not concern us: HB is exactly the hypothesis that \(|\varphi|<1\) there, in particular \(\varphi\) is finite, i.e. \(E_h\) itself is zero-free on \(\mathbb C^+\)). So \(\varphi\) is a Schur function: holomorphic, \(|\varphi|\le1\) (indeed \(<1\)) on \(\mathbb C^+\). The Szegő kernel \(S(w,z) = \frac{i}{2\pi(z-\bar w)} = \int_0^\infty e^{izt}\overline{e^{iwt}}\,dt\) is positive definite on \(\mathbb C^+\) (it is a Gram kernel of the vectors \(t\mapsto e^{izt}\big|_{t>0} \in L^2(0,\infty)\), convergent precisely for \(\mathrm{Im}\,z>0\)). Multiplication by \(\varphi\) is then a contraction \(M_\varphi\) on the reproducing kernel Hilbert space of \(S\) (the Hardy space \(H^2(\mathbb C^+)\)), so \(I - M_\varphi M_\varphi^{*} \succeq 0\), and its Gram-kernel representation \(\langle (I-M_\varphi M_\varphi^{*})S_w, S_z\rangle = \big(1-\varphi(z)\overline{\varphi(w)}\big)S(w,z)\) is a positive semi-definite kernel. Multiplying by \(E_h(z)\overline{E_h(w)}\) is a congruence (does not change definiteness), and gives exactly \[ E_h(z)\overline{E_h(w)}\big(1-\varphi(z)\overline{\varphi(w)}\big)S(w,z) = \big[E_h(z)\overline{E_h(w)} - E_h^{*}(z)\overline{E_h^{*}(w)}\big]\,\frac{i}{2\pi(z-\bar w)} = K_h(w,z). \] Hence \(K_h \succeq 0\). \(\blacksquare\)

Step \((3)\Rightarrow(1)\).

If \(K_h\succeq0\) on \(\mathbb C^+\times\mathbb C^+\) for every \(h>0\), then all zeros of \(\Xi\) are real.

Proof. The diagonal of a positive semi-definite kernel is non-negative: \(K_h(z,z)\ge0\) gives \(|E_h(z)|\ge|E_h^{*}(z)|\), i.e. \(|\Xi(z+ih)| \ge |\Xi(z-ih)|\) for all \(z\in\mathbb C^+\), for every \(h>0\). Suppose \(w\) is a zero of \(\Xi\) with \(\mathrm{Im}\,w > h\). Set \(z = w-ih \in \mathbb C^+\) (since \(\mathrm{Im}\,z = \mathrm{Im}\,w - h>0\)). Then \(\Xi(z+ih) = \Xi(w) = 0\), so the inequality forces \(\Xi(z-ih) = \Xi(w-2ih) = 0\) as well: \(w-2ih\) is again a zero of \(\Xi\), provided \(\mathrm{Im}(w-2ih) \) is compared correctly — we only used \(z\in\mathbb C^+\), i.e. \(\mathrm{Im}\,w>h\), with no constraint on where \(w-2ih\) lands.

Now suppose, for contradiction, that RH fails. By the conjugation symmetry of the zero set, there is a zero \(\gamma_0\) with \(c := \mathrm{Im}\,\gamma_0 > 0\). Fix any \(K\in\mathbb N\) and set \(h = c/(2K)\). Apply the displacement step above repeatedly, starting from \(w_0=\gamma_0\): as long as the current zero \(w_j\) has \(\mathrm{Im}\,w_j > h\), we obtain a new zero \(w_{j+1} = w_j - 2ih\). Since \(\mathrm{Im}\,w_j = c - 2jh = c(1-j/K)\), the hypothesis \(\mathrm{Im}\,w_j>h\) holds for \(j=0,1,\dots,K-1\), producing zeros \(w_0,w_1,\dots,w_K\) with \(\mathrm{Im}\,w_j = c(1-j/K)\), all sharing the same real part \(\mathrm{Re}\,\gamma_0\) (each displacement is purely vertical).

Doing this for every \(K\in\mathbb N\) produces, for each \(K\), a chain of \(K+1\) zeros on the vertical segment \(\{\mathrm{Re}\,\gamma_0\} \times [0,c]\), with consecutive imaginary parts spaced by \(c/K \to 0\). Since \(\Xi\) is a fixed nonzero entire function, its zero set is discrete: it has a positive minimum spacing on any compact set (e.g. the segment \(\{\mathrm{Re}\,\gamma_0\}\times[-1,c+1]\), compact since \(\Xi\) has finitely many zeros of bounded imaginary part locally — indeed zeros are isolated, full stop). Taking \(K\) large enough that \(c/K\) is smaller than that minimum spacing already contradicts discreteness (distinct chain zeros \(w_j\ne w_{j+1}\) since \(h>0\), yet they can be forced arbitrarily close). Hence no such \(\gamma_0\) exists, and RH holds. \(\blacksquare\)

5. Discussion: what this equivalence is, and is not

What it is. A correct, fully proved de Branges-theoretic reformulation of RH, replacing the false claim (refuted in the companion errata) that \(\Xi\) itself is a structure function. It shows precisely how much non-reality is needed to escape Lemma N3 of research-findings.html (real entire functions are never HB): an arbitrarily small vertical shift \(h>0\) suffices, in the sense that HB-ness of every such shift is exactly equivalent to RH.

What it is not. The content is close to a direct restatement. Unwinding the proof of \((3)\Rightarrow(1)\): the positivity of \(K_h\) is used only through its diagonal inequality \(|\Xi(z+ih)|\ge|\Xi(z-ih)|\), which is itself little more than "no zero has imaginary part exceeding \(h\), for arbitrarily small \(h\)" dressed in kernel language — i.e. close to a paraphrase of "all zeros are real" rather than a new constraint on \(\Xi\) derived from deeper structure. We record it because (a) it is the *correct* fix to a real error present in the literature under review, (b) it cleanly demonstrates the general mechanism — Schur-class contraction giving kernel positivity — that appears in the genuinely substantial equivalences of §6, and (c) the numerical form (§7) gives a checkable, falsifiable statement at each finite \(h\), unlike the un-shiftable original claim.

Contrast with the de Bruijn–Newman constant. Do not confuse \(E_h(z)=\Xi(z+ih)\) (a rigid translation of the argument) with the de Bruijn–Newman deformation \(\Xi_t\), defined via the heat-flow \(\Xi_t(z) = \sum \big(\text{Fourier coefficients of }\Xi\text{ damped by } e^{tk^2}\big)\) (equivalently a genuine convolution smoothing of \(\Xi\) along the real axis, not a shift off it). The de Bruijn–Newman constant \(\Lambda\) is defined so that \(\Xi_t\) has only real zeros iff \(t\ge\Lambda\); RH is the (open) statement \(\Lambda\le0\), while \(\Lambda\ge0\) is a proven theorem (Rodgers–Tao, 2020), and \(\Lambda\le1/2\) is proven (Polymath15, building on Newman's original bound). That theory is substantially deeper than Theorem 3.1 here — it is a one-parameter family of genuinely different entire functions approaching \(\Xi\) as \(t\to0^+\), with real analytic techniques (the heat equation \(\partial_t\Xi_t = \partial_x^2 \Xi_t\)) controlling how zeros move. The present note's shift \(E_h\) is a much shallower device: a translation off the real axis makes non-reality visible, but supplies no mechanism controlling *where* zeros can be. We flag the distinction so the two are never conflated.

6. Relation to the other RH-equivalent criteria

Theorem 3.1 sits alongside the other unconditionally proven RH-equivalences catalogued in research-findings.html §7: Weil positivity (§7.2 there), Li's criterion (§7.3), and the open status of Connes' global trace formula. All are walls at the same frontier. The Schur-function-contraction mechanism used in Step \((2)\Rightarrow(3)\) above is the same one that underlies Weil's original quadratic-form positivity argument; the companion note prime-kernel.html constructs the "geometric side" Hilbert space (of prime Dirichlet series) whose boundary quadratic form is exactly the Weil functional — a natural next question is whether that boundary form can be related directly to \(K_h\) as \(h\downarrow0\), which we leave open.

All five sample points in §§7.1–7.2 lie within the region where RH is computationally verified (\(|\mathrm{Im}\,z|\) small; zeros verified to height \(3\times10^{12}\), Platt–Trudgian 2021), so consistency here is expected and does not constitute independent evidence — it is a correctness check on the formulas and the code, not a test of RH. 8. Summary

The Riemann Hypothesis is unconditionally equivalent to the Hermite–Biehler property of every vertical shift \(E_h(z)=\Xi(z+ih)\), \(h>0\), equivalently to positive semi-definiteness of the associated de Branges kernel \(K_h\) on \(\mathbb C^+\times\mathbb C^+\). This is proved in full in both directions (§4), corrects a specific false claim in the literature under review (that \(\Xi\) itself is Hermite–Biehler), and is verified numerically. As with every other unconditional RH-equivalence, it marks the frontier rather than crossing it: any attempted proof along these lines must, at some point, inject the positivity directly — there is no way to derive \(K_h\succeq0\) for all \(h>0\) from weaker structural facts about \(\Xi\) without already encoding RH.
