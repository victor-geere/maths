# PSC2-WP14 — the three escape routes from the quantum-graph no-go (G5)

*Status: **exploratory** tier. The no-go itself is proven
([S00 §10](../sources/PSC2-S00-verified-foundation.md)): closed, $s$-independent-unitary
models are dead (X6). Three doors remain; each gets one small, concrete first experiment in
the existing lab framework — nothing more until one shows signal.*

## Door 1 — energy-dependent scattering $\Sigma(s)$

Fit the minimal $s$-dependence restoring the $\Lambda$-support on $m \le 100$; compare its
analytic shape to the archimedean $\Gamma$-factor and the Connes–Moscovici prolate structure
(the natural home of energy dependence). *Sharp question:* is the required $\Sigma(s)$
inner-function-like — unitary on the critical line, contractive off it? That property is
Hermite–Biehler-shaped: **if it appears, this door has walked to the wall (W5), as it must** —
record the arrival point precisely and stop.

## Door 2 — open graphs / resonances

Make the flower lossy (subunitary $\Sigma$); zeros as resonances of an open system
(de Branges/HB direction). First experiment: can a rank-one opening reproduce the first
Weil-term signs? (Lee–Yang reading: openness = boundary field.)

## Door 3 — pseudo-orbit interference

Compute the size of the mixed-orbit sector that must cancel at each length $\log m$; quantify
the "interference budget" the arithmetic demands as a function of cutoff (Berry–Keating
resurgence heuristics as guide). Deliverable is a *number table*, not a claim.

## Rules

All tagged **exploratory**; I0 binding (in particular: fits in Door 1 are fits to the
*prime side* $\Lambda(m)/\sqrt m$ — proven data — never to zeros or $N(T)$). Findings feed
design constraints back into WP12; no door is a route to RH and none may be priced as one.

## Falsifier

Per door: Door 1 — no $s$-dependence within a natural function class restores the support;
Door 2 — rank-one openings cannot match the sign pattern; Door 3 — the budget grows faster
than any plausible interference mechanism supplies. Each closure is a finding.
