0. Summary of findings

The series (barry-keating-hamiltonian-sieve.html, barry-keating-completing.html, barry-keating-hp-1.html … hp-7.html) claims, in hp-3 and again in hp-7, a complete proof of the Riemann Hypothesis. The claim is incorrect. Three independent, elementary obstructions (§1: Lemmas L1–L3) each break the chain before it starts:

  1. L1. Every eigenvalue of every finite-stage operator \(H_n^{\mathrm{osc}}\) lies in a fixed triangle of diameter \(<1.2\) near the origin; the eigenvalues can never approach the Riemann zeros \(\gamma_1 = 14.13\ldots,\;\gamma_2 = 21.02\ldots\), under any hypothesis.
  2. L2. The "de Branges kernel of \(\Xi\)" used throughout is identically zero, because \(\Xi\) is a real entire function; a real entire function is never a de Branges structure function. The "limiting de Branges space" of the series is \(\{0\}\).
  3. L3. The finite prime kernels \(K_n\) converge to \(0\) on the strip \(|\mathrm{Im}(\lambda-\bar\mu)|<1\) and diverge below it; there is no nonzero limit kernel.

Beyond these, the two numerical showcases are unreproducible from the listed code: the eigenvalue table of completing.html §4 is contradicted by running its own script (actual max error \(30.2\) at \(N=120\), claimed \(0.0436\); appendix N.1), and the GUE "unfolding" of hp-6 §2 maps any spectrum to exactly equal spacings (appendix N.2).

What survives is genuinely worth keeping, and this addendum completes it: the sieve lemma (A.1), the equidistribution of \(\{p/M_n\}\) with a corrected proof (A.2), the exact spectral theory of the Berry–Keating generator (A.3), the Guinand–Weil explicit formula in a normalisation verified here to \(10^{-31}\) (A.4), the honest content of the moment argument together with a proof that the moment strategy cannot detect reality of zeros (A.5), and the honest de Branges statement: a Hermite–Biehler positivity criterion equivalent to RH (A.6). Sections A.7–A.9 give the accurate status of the adelic, spectral-triple and GUE claims with literature. Section 5 states precisely the wall every route of this series runs into. Nothing in the series, and nothing in this addendum, proves the Riemann Hypothesis; it remains open.

1. Three master obstructions

Lemma L1 (spectral confinement of the compressions). proven

Work in the corrected model of Erratum E1, in which \(\{e_p\}_{p\le M_n}\) is orthonormal (circle of circumference \(M_n\)) and \(\widehat H_n e_p = (p/M_n + i/2)\,e_p\), so \(\widehat H_n\) is normal with spectrum in the segment \(S = [0,1]+\tfrac i2\). Let \(V:\mathcal H_n \to \mathbb C^{N}\) be any linear contraction (\(\|V\|\le 1\)) — in particular the oscillator embedding \((V_n)_{m,p} = \langle \psi_m, U e_p\rangle\) of sieve §5.3 / completing §3.2, which is (projection) ∘ (unitary) ∘ (inclusion). Then every eigenvalue \(\lambda\) of \(H^{\mathrm{osc}} = V \widehat H_n V^{*}\) lies in the triangle \[ T \;=\; \mathrm{conv}\{\,0,\; \tfrac i2,\; 1+\tfrac i2\,\}, \qquad\text{so } |\lambda| \le \tfrac{\sqrt5}{2} < 1.12 . \]

Proof. Let \(H^{\mathrm{osc}}v = \lambda v\) with \(\|v\| = 1\), and set \(u = V^{*}v\), so \(\|u\|\le1\). Then \(\lambda = \langle v, V\widehat H_n V^{*} v\rangle = \langle u, \widehat H_n u\rangle\). If \(u = 0\), \(\lambda = 0 \in T\). Otherwise \(\lambda = \|u\|^2 \langle \hat u, \widehat H_n \hat u\rangle\) with \(\hat u = u/\|u\|\), and since the numerical range of a normal operator is the closed convex hull of its spectrum, \(\langle\hat u, \widehat H_n \hat u\rangle \in S\). Hence \(\lambda \in \{ t w : t\in[0,1],\, w \in S\} = T\). \(\blacksquare\)

Under the documents' literal reading on \([0,1)\) the \(e_p\) are far from orthonormal (Gram \(\langle e_p,e_q\rangle = e^{\pi i(p-q)/M_n}\,\mathrm{sinc}(\pi(p-q)/M_n)\approx 1\) for \(|p-q|\ll M_n\)) and the claimed eigenvalue relation fails before L1 is even needed; see E1.

Consequences of L1. (i) Sieve Theorem 6.1 is false even assuming RH: no eigenvalue of \(H_n^{\mathrm{osc}}\) can leave \(|\lambda| < 1.12\), while \(\gamma_1 = 14.1347\ldots\). (ii) Sieve Theorem 7.1 and completing Theorem 3.1 are false: all \(N_n\) roots of \(\Xi_n(\lambda) = \det(\lambda I - H_n^{\mathrm{osc}})\) lie in \(T\), so for \(|\lambda|\ge2\), \(|\Xi_n(\lambda)| \ge (|\lambda| - \tfrac{\sqrt5}{2})^{N_n} \to \infty\) while \(\Xi(\lambda)\) is finite; no locally uniform convergence is possible. (iii) hp-1 Lemma 2.1 fails at the single point \(\lambda = -1\): the trace side has real part \(\le -\tfrac{4}{17}N_n \to -\infty\) (each \(w_j = -1-\lambda_j\) has \(\mathrm{Re}\,w_j \in [-2,-1]\), \(|w_j|^2 \le \tfrac{17}{4}\)), while the right-hand side is bounded by \(1 + o(1)\) in modulus.

Lemma L2 (the claimed kernel vanishes identically). proven

\(\Xi(\lambda) = \xi(\tfrac12 + i\lambda)\) is real entire: \(\overline{\Xi(\bar\lambda)} = \Xi(\lambda)\). Consequently the kernel used in sieve §10.2, completing Lemma 2.2, hp-3 §§1,3 and hp-5 §1, \[ K_\Xi(\lambda,\mu) \;=\; \frac{\Xi(\lambda)\overline{\Xi(\bar\mu)} - \overline{\Xi(\bar\lambda)}\Xi(\mu)}{2\pi i(\bar\mu - \lambda)}, \] is identically zero, and the associated "de Branges space" is \(\{0\}\). Moreover no real entire function \(E\) is a de Branges structure function, since reality forces \(|E(x-iy)| = |E(x+iy)|\) for all \(x,y\), contradicting the required strict inequality \(|E(x-iy)| < |E(x+iy)|\;(y>0)\).

Proof. Reality of \(\xi\)'s Maclaurin coefficients on the critical line is classical (e.g. Titchmarsh §2.1: \(\Xi\) is the Riemann \(\Xi\)-function, real and even). Then \(\overline{\Xi(\bar\mu)} = \Xi(\mu)\) and \(\overline{\Xi(\bar\lambda)} = \Xi(\lambda)\), so the numerator is \(\Xi(\lambda)\Xi(\mu) - \Xi(\lambda)\Xi(\mu) = 0\). For the second claim, \(|E(x-iy)| = |\overline{E(x-iy)}| = |E(\overline{x-iy})|^{\,\overline{\phantom{x}}} = |E(x+iy)|\). \(\blacksquare\)

Numerical confirmation (appendix N.4): the numerator is \(<10^{-31}\) at generic complex points where \(|\Xi| \approx 0.48\). Note also that the displayed formula misquotes de Branges: the correct reproducing kernel is the sesquilinear \(K(w,z) = \big[E(z)\overline{E(w)} - E^{*}(z)\overline{E^{*}(w)}\big]\, i/\big(2\pi(z-\bar w)\big)\), \(E^{*}(z) := \overline{E(\bar z)}\) — which also degenerates when \(E^{*} = E\). The honest de Branges statement for \(\Xi\) is Proposition A.6 below, and it is equivalent to RH.

Lemma L3 (the prime kernels have no nonzero limit). proven

Let \(K_n(\lambda,\mu) = \frac{1}{k_n}\sum_{p\le M_n} p^{-1+i(\lambda-\bar\mu)}\) and write \(u = \lambda - \bar\mu\). Then, as \(n\to\infty\): (a) uniformly on \(\{\mathrm{Im}\,u \ge -1+\varepsilon\}\cap\{|u|\le R\}\), \(K_n \to 0\); (b) for \(\mathrm{Im}\,u < -1\), \(|K_n| \to \infty\). In particular sieve Theorem 10.1 and completing Theorem 2.1 are false, and on the strip the limit kernel is \(0 = K_\Xi\): the "identification \(K_\infty = K_\Xi\)" is the true but vacuous statement \(0 = 0\).

Proof. \(|p^{iu}| = p^{-\mathrm{Im}\,u}\). For \(\mathrm{Im}\,u \ge 0\): \(\sum_{p\le M} p^{-1-\mathrm{Im}\,u} \le \sum_{p \le M} p^{-1} = \log\log M + O(1)\), and \(k_n \sim M/\log M\), so \(|K_n| \ll (\log M \log\log M)/M\). For \(\mathrm{Im}\,u = -\delta\), \(0<\delta<1\): by Chebyshev bounds and partial summation \(\sum_{p\le M} p^{-1+\delta} \asymp M^{\delta}/(\delta\log M)\), so \(|K_n| \asymp M^{\delta-1}/\delta \to 0\), uniformly for \(\delta \le 1-\varepsilon\). For \(\delta > 1\) the same estimate gives \(|K_n| \asymp M^{\delta-1} \to \infty\). \(\blacksquare\)

The specific error in the documents' Hilbert–Schmidt argument: the bound "\(\iint_\Omega p^{i\lambda}q^{-i\bar\mu}\,d\lambda\,d\mu = O(1/\log(p/q))\)" is false on complex domains, where \(|p^{i\lambda}|\) grows like \(p^{|\mathrm{Im}\lambda|}\); there is also an expansion of \(|\sum_p(\cdot)|^2\) without cross terms. Sieve §10.2 Step 1 itself concedes that the pointwise limit does not exist, then reintroduces the same kernel two displays later. Numerics: appendix N.3.

3. Addendum: completed and corrected results

A.1 The sieve lemma proven

Lemma A.1 (sieve §2.1 / completing §1.1, completed).

Let \(I_n = [2^n, 2^{n+1}-1]\), let \(\mathcal P_n\) be the primes \(<2^n\), and let \(G_n\) be the set of \(x\in I_n\) expressible as \(x = p_1p_2\cdots p_k\) with \(p_i \in \mathcal P_n\) (repetitions allowed) and \(k\ge2\). Then \(G_n\) is exactly the set of composite numbers in \(I_n\).

Proof. A product of \(k\ge2\) primes is composite, so \(G_n \subseteq \{\text{composites}\}\). Conversely let \(x\in I_n\) be composite and let \(x = q_1\cdots q_k\) (\(k\ge2\), primes with multiplicity). Each \(q_i\) satisfies \(q_i = x/m\) for the integer \(m = \prod_{j\ne i}q_j \ge 2\), hence \(q_i \le x/2 < 2^{n+1}/2 = 2^n\), i.e. \(q_i \in \mathcal P_n\). \(\blacksquare\)

Repetitions are necessary (\(4 = 2\cdot2 \in I_2\)); the original statement should be read with multisets. By induction the primes below \(2^{n}\) are available at stage \(n\), so the recursion is well-founded. This is the one fully correct structural claim of the base document.

A.2 Equidistribution of the normalised primes, with a corrected proof proven

Lemma A.2 (sieve Lemma 3.1 / completing Lemma 1.1, corrected).

For each fixed \(k\in\mathbb Z\setminus\{0\}\), \[ \widehat\mu_n(k) = \frac1{k_n}\sum_{p\le M_n} e^{2\pi i k p/M_n} \;=\; O_k\!\Big(\frac1{\log M_n}\Big) \longrightarrow 0, \] and \(\widehat\mu_n(0)=1\); hence \(\mu_n \to\) Lebesgue measure weak-*.

Proof. Write \(S = \sum_{p\le M}e(kp/M) = \int_{2^-}^{M} e(kt/M)\,d\pi(t)\) and \(\pi(t) = \mathrm{Li}(t) + E(t)\) with \(E(t) \ll t\,e^{-c\sqrt{\log t}}\) (de la Vallée Poussin). The error part contributes, after integrating by parts, \(\ll (1+|k|)\,M e^{-c\sqrt{\log M}}\). For the main part substitute \(t = Ms\): \(\int_{2/M}^{1} e(ks)\,\frac{M\,ds}{\log(Ms)}\). The range \(s \le M^{-1/2}\) contributes \(O(\sqrt M)\). On \(s \ge M^{-1/2}\), where \(|\log s| \le \tfrac12\log M\), expand \(\frac1{\log(Ms)} = \frac1{\log M}\big(1 - \frac{\log s}{\log M} + O\big(\tfrac{\log^2 s}{\log^2 M}\big)\big)\). Since \(\int_0^1 e(ks)\,ds = 0\) for \(k \in \mathbb Z\setminus\{0\}\), the first term contributes \(O(\sqrt M)\); the second is \(\ll_k M/\log^2 M\) (as \(\int_0^1|\log s|\,ds = 1\)); the third is \(\ll M/\log^3 M\). Altogether \(S \ll_k M/\log^2 M\), and dividing by \(k_n \sim M/\log M\) gives the claim. Weyl's criterion (trigonometric polynomials are dense in \(C(\mathbb T)\), total masses are \(1\)) yields weak-* convergence. \(\blacksquare\)

Erratum in the cited proof. Both documents derive this from "Vinogradov's bound \(\sum_{p\le x} e(\alpha p) \ll_A x/\log^A x\) for \(0<|\alpha|\le\tfrac12\)". No such bound holds for all such \(\alpha\): at \(\alpha = \tfrac13\), by the prime number theorem in arithmetic progressions and the Ramanujan sum \(c_3(1)=\mu(3)\), \(\sum_{p\le x} e(p/3) \sim -\tfrac12\,x/\log x\). Vinogradov-type savings require \(\alpha\) on a minor arc; the frequencies \(k/M_n\) here are major-arc rationals, and the correct route is the PNT + partial summation argument above — which is fortunately all the lemma needs.

A.3 The Berry–Keating generator, exactly proven

Proposition A.3 (replaces sieve §5/Thm 5.1's picture; fixes hp-5 §2.1).

On \(L^2(\mathbb R_+, dx)\) the operator \(H = -i\big(x\frac{d}{dx} + \tfrac12\big)\) is essentially self-adjoint on \(C_c^\infty(0,\infty)\). The map \((U_1 f)(u) = e^{u/2} f(e^u)\) is unitary onto \(L^2(\mathbb R, du)\) and \(U_1 H U_1^{-1} = -i\,\frac{d}{du}\). Consequently \(H\) has purely absolutely continuous, simple spectrum equal to \(\mathbb R\), and the normalised Mellin transform \[ (\mathcal M f)(\lambda) = \frac1{\sqrt{2\pi}}\int_0^\infty f(x)\,x^{-\frac12 - i\lambda}\,dx \] is unitary from \(L^2(\mathbb R_+, dx)\) onto \(L^2(\mathbb R, d\lambda)\) with \(\mathcal M H \mathcal M^{-1} = \) multiplication by \(\lambda\).

Proof. Unitarity of \(U_1\): \(\int_{\mathbb R}|e^{u/2}f(e^u)|^2du = \int_0^\infty |f(x)|^2\,dx\). Conjugation: with \(f(x) = x^{-1/2}g(\log x)\) one computes \(x f'(x) + \tfrac12 f(x) = x^{-1/2}g'(\log x)\), so \(U_1(Hf) = -ig'\). The operator \(-i\,d/du\) on \(C_c^\infty(\mathbb R)\) has deficiency indices \((0,0)\) — the equations \(-i\psi' = \pm i\psi\) have solutions \(e^{\mp u}\notin L^2(\mathbb R)\) — hence is essentially self-adjoint, with Fourier transform giving multiplication by \(\lambda\): purely absolutely continuous simple spectrum \(\mathbb R\). Unitary conjugation transports all statements; \(\mathcal M = \mathcal F U_1\) is the stated Mellin transform. \(\blacksquare\)

Remarks. (i) The ubiquitous "\(+i/2\)" of the series is the Jacobian of \(L^2(dx) \leftrightarrow L^2(dx/x)\), nothing else: on \(L^2(dx/x)\) the generator is \(-ix\partial_x\) and the Mellin kernel is \(x^{-i\lambda}\,dx/x\). hp-5 §2.1 mixes the two normalisations (its kernel is unitary from \(L^2(dx)\), not \(L^2(dx/x)\)). (ii) The spectrum is a continuum. Any claim of discrete spectrum — let alone at the Riemann zeros — requires changing the Hilbert space or boundary conditions (Berry–Keating's Planck-cell regularisation is semiclassical counting, not a self-adjoint realisation; Sierra–Rodríguez-Laguna's \(x(p + \ell_p^2/p)\) achieves a discrete spectrum matching only the smooth zero count; Connes' construction changes the space to the adele class quotient). This is where the entire difficulty of Hilbert–Pólya lives, and no finite-rank compression of the continuum can create it (Lemma L1).

A.4 The Guinand–Weil explicit formula, stated and verified proven (classical)

Theorem A.4 (Guinand 1948; Weil 1952; Barner 1981; Iwaniec–Kowalski Thm 5.12).

Let \(h\) be even, holomorphic on \(|\mathrm{Im}\,z| \le \tfrac12+\delta\), with \(h(z) \ll (1+|z|)^{-2-\delta}\) there, and let \(g(u) = \frac1{2\pi}\int_{\mathbb R} h(r)e^{-iur}\,dr\). Then, with \(\rho = \tfrac12 + i\gamma_\rho\) running over the nontrivial zeros of \(\zeta\) (with multiplicity), \[ \sum_\rho h(\gamma_\rho) = h\big(\tfrac i2\big) + h\big(-\tfrac i2\big) - g(0)\log\pi + \frac1{2\pi}\int_{\mathbb R} h(r)\,\mathrm{Re}\,\psi\big(\tfrac14 + \tfrac{ir}2\big)\,dr - 2\sum_{n\ge2} \frac{\Lambda(n)}{\sqrt n}\, g(\log n), \] where \(\psi = \Gamma'/\Gamma\). The identity is unconditional.

Derivation sketch and verification. Integrate \(h\big({-i(s-\tfrac12)}\big)\,\tfrac{\xi'}{\xi}(s)\) over a rectangle containing the critical strip; residues at the zeros give the left side; \(\xi = \tfrac12 s(s-1)\pi^{-s/2}\Gamma(s/2)\zeta(s)\) splits the right side into the pole terms \(h(\pm i/2)\), the archimedean digamma term with the \(-\log\pi\) contribution, and the prime side via \(-\zeta'/\zeta(s) = \sum \Lambda(n)n^{-s}\). Full details in Iwaniec–Kowalski, Thm 5.12. The normalisation above was verified in 30-digit arithmetic with \(h(r) = e^{-(r/6)^2}\), \(g(u) = \frac{3}{\sqrt\pi}e^{-9u^2}\): both sides equal \(0.0077863602482884239503\ldots\), difference \(9.4\times10^{-32}\) (appendix N.5).

This — a distributional trace identity, not an operator equality — is the true bridge between primes and zeros that hp-1 §3 gestures at. Its positivity form is exactly the wall of §5 below.

A.5 What is true in Task 2, and why the moment strategy cannot work proven

Proposition A.5.1 (unconditional distribution of scaled ordinates).

Let \(0<\gamma_1\le\gamma_2\le\cdots\) be the ordinates of the nontrivial zeros in the upper half-strip (no hypothesis on their real parts). Then the empirical measures of \(\{\gamma_j/\gamma_N\}_{j\le N}\) converge weak-* to the uniform distribution on \([0,1]\); the symmetrised measures converge to uniform on \([-1,1]\), with even moments \(m_{2k} = \frac1{2k+1}\), odd moments \(0\). The moment sequence satisfies Carleman's condition trivially (\(m_{2k}\le1\)) and is determinate.

Proof. Riemann–von Mangoldt (unconditional): \(N(T) = \frac{T}{2\pi}\log\frac{T}{2\pi e} + \frac78 + O(\log T)\). For fixed \(t\in(0,1]\), \[ \frac{\#\{j\le N: \gamma_j \le t\gamma_N\}}{N} = \frac{N(t\gamma_N)}{N(\gamma_N)} = t\Big(1 + \frac{\log t}{\log(\gamma_N/2\pi e)}\Big) + O\Big(\frac{\log\gamma_N}{N}\Big) \to t . \] Convergence of distribution functions at every point gives weak-* convergence; moments follow by bounded convergence. Determinacy: compactly supported measures are determined by their moments (Weierstrass), and Carleman holds a fortiori. \(\blacksquare\)

The \(1/\log\) distortion explains the slow numerics (measured \(\frac1N\sum(\gamma_j/\gamma_N)^2 = 0.443\) at \(N=40\) vs limit \(1/3\); appendix N.6). With hp-2's normalisation \(S_n = N/\log N\) the same statement holds with limit uniform on \([0,2\pi]\) — their claimed limiting moments were the right guess attached to a divergent quantity (E12).

Proposition A.5.2 (moments cannot see reality of support).

There exist conjugation-symmetric probability measures \(\nu_1,\nu_2\) on \(\mathbb C\) with \(\int z^k d\nu_1 = \int z^k d\nu_2\) for all \(k\ge0\), such that \(\nu_1\) is supported on \(\mathbb R\) and \(\nu_2\) charges no point of \(\mathbb R\): take \(\nu_1 = \delta_0\) and \(\nu_2 = \) the uniform measure on the unit circle, whose moments are both \(\delta_{k,0}\). Consequently no theorem of the form "the holomorphic moment sequence \(\frac1N\mathrm{Tr}\,(H_n^{\mathrm{osc}})^k\) is real and determinate, therefore the limiting support is real" can be valid, and hp-2 §5 is irreparable as a strategy. What does force real spectra is symmetry of the operator (\(\langle Hu,v\rangle = \langle u,Hv\rangle\)) — i.e. self-adjointness, the original Hilbert–Pólya requirement.

A.6 The honest de Branges statement: a positivity criterion equivalent to RH proven equivalence

Proposition A.6 (replaces hp-3).

For \(h>0\) let \(E_h(z) = \Xi(z + ih)\), \(E_h^{*}(z) = \overline{E_h(\bar z)} = \Xi(z-ih)\), and let \[ K_h(w,z) = \frac{i\,\big[E_h(z)\overline{E_h(w)} - E_h^{*}(z)\overline{E_h^{*}(w)}\big]}{2\pi\,(z - \bar w)} . \] The following are equivalent.

  1. The Riemann Hypothesis (all zeros of \(\Xi\) are real).
  2. For every \(h>0\), \(E_h\) is a Hermite–Biehler function: \(|E_h^{*}(z)| < |E_h(z)|\) for all \(z\in\mathbb C^+\).
  3. For every \(h>0\), the kernel \(K_h\) is positive semi-definite on \(\mathbb C^+\times\mathbb C^+\).

Proof. We use the unconditional even Hadamard product: \(\Xi\) is even, real, of order \(1\), \(\Xi(0) = \xi(\tfrac12)\ne0\), with zeros \(\{\pm\gamma_j\}\) (listed with multiplicity, \(|\mathrm{Im}\,\gamma_j|<\tfrac12\), \(\sum|\gamma_j|^{-2}<\infty\)); pairing the genus-one factors of \(\pm\gamma_j\) and using evenness to kill the exponential, \(\Xi(z) = \Xi(0)\prod_j\big(1 - z^2/\gamma_j^2\big)\) (Titchmarsh §2.12).

(1) ⟹ (2). With all \(\gamma_j>0\) real, for \(z = x+iy\), \(y>0\): \[ \left|\frac{E_h^{*}(z)}{E_h(z)}\right| = \prod_j \frac{|\gamma_j - z + ih|\,|\gamma_j + z - ih|}{|\gamma_j - z - ih|\,|\gamma_j + z + ih|}, \] and \(|\gamma - z + ih|^2 = (\gamma - x)^2 + (h-y)^2 < (\gamma-x)^2 + (h+y)^2 = |\gamma - z - ih|^2\), likewise for the second pair; every factor is \(<1\) and the product converges, so the ratio is \(<1\). (Zeros of \(E_h\) sit at \(\gamma_j - ih \notin \mathbb C^+\).)

(2) ⟹ (3). Let \(\varphi = E_h^{*}/E_h\), holomorphic on \(\mathbb C^+\) with \(|\varphi| < 1\) by (2). The Szegő kernel \(S(w,z) = \frac{i}{2\pi(z-\bar w)}\) is positive definite on \(\mathbb C^+\) (reproducing kernel of \(H^2\)), and for a Schur function \(\varphi\) the Pick-type kernel \((1 - \varphi(z)\overline{\varphi(w)})\,S(w,z)\) is positive semi-definite (multiplication by \(\varphi\) is a contraction on \(H^2\), so \(\langle (I - M_\varphi M_\varphi^{*}) S_w, S_z\rangle\) is a Gram-type kernel). Multiplying by \(E_h(z)\overline{E_h(w)}\) — a congruence — gives exactly \(K_h \succeq 0\).

(3) ⟹ (1). The diagonal of \(K_h\) gives \(|\Xi(z-ih)| \le |\Xi(z+ih)|\) for all \(\mathrm{Im}\,z>0\); equivalently: if \(w\) is a zero of \(\Xi\) with \(\mathrm{Im}\,w > h\), then \(w - 2ih\) is also a zero (take \(z = w - ih\); the majorant vanishes). Suppose RH fails: by conjugation symmetry pick a zero \(\gamma_0\) with \(c = \mathrm{Im}\,\gamma_0 > 0\). Fix \(K\in\mathbb N\) and take \(h = c/(2K)\): applying the step \(K\) times produces zeros at \(\gamma_0 - 2ijh\), i.e. with imaginary parts \(c(1 - j/K)\) for \(j = 0,1,\dots,K\) (the step is legitimate while \(c(1-j/K) > h\), i.e. \(j \le K-1\)). Letting \(K\) run, \(\Xi\) acquires zeros with imaginary parts dense in \([0,c]\) on the vertical segment through \(\mathrm{Re}\,\gamma_0\) — impossible for a nonzero entire function, whose zeros are isolated. Hence all zeros are real. \(\blacksquare\)

Reading. This is what hp-3 could legitimately have proved — and it shows the positivity of a de Branges-type kernel attached to \(\Xi\) is not a stepping stone to RH: it is RH. Any argument that "the limit of positive finite kernels is positive, hence RH" must fail, and does: the finite kernels of the series converge to \(0\) (L3). For genuine, rigorous de Branges-space realisations connected to \(\zeta\), see Burnol's Sonine spaces and Lagarias' survey (references).

A.7 Status of the adelic claims (Tasks 4, 5, 7) status map

Proven (by others), correctly stated. (i) Tate: Mellin–Plancherel on \(C_{\mathbb Q}\); for the basic \(K\)-invariant vector with Gaussian archimedean component, the zeta integral is \(\Lambda(\tfrac12+i\lambda) = \pi^{-s/2}\Gamma(s/2)\zeta(s)\big|_{s=\frac12+i\lambda}\) — an \(L^2\) identity (this fixes hp-5 §5, whose \(\mathbf 1_K\)-integral diverges). (ii) Connes (1999): the semi-local trace formula: for each finite set of places \(S\), the scaling action on the \(S\)-adelic quotient satisfies a Guillemin-type trace formula whose geometric side is precisely the corresponding part of Weil's explicit formula; and an unconditional absorption-spectrum interpretation of the zeros in Sobolev-conditioned function spaces. (iii) Meyer (2005): an unconditional spectral realisation of all zeros, with multiplicity, by the flow on nuclear function spaces over the adele class space — a space deliberately without Hilbert-space positivity, which is why no reality conclusion follows. This is the correct version of hp-4 §4.1's assertion, and it demonstrates exactly what is missing. (iv) Connes–Consani (2021–): Weil positivity at the archimedean place via the quantised calculus; ζ-cycles/prolate-operator realisations of low zeros.

Open, and equivalent to RH. The global trace formula in the Hilbert space setting — equivalently Weil positivity (§5). hp-4 §4.1's "\(D_K\) has pure point spectrum equal to the zeros, compact resolvent" asserts this open statement as established.

Corrections of record (E14). \(N(T) = \frac{T}{2\pi}\log\frac{T}{2\pi e} + \frac78 + O(\log T)\) is polynomially bounded; \(\sum_j\gamma_j^{-p}\) converges iff \(p>1\); on the continuous subspace \(|D|^{-p}\) is not compact for any \(p\); and the trace of the zero spectrum, \(Z(s) = \sum_{\gamma>0}\gamma^{-s}\), is Voros' secondary zeta function — meromorphic with a double-pole structure at \(s=1\) inherited from \(N(T)\) — not \(\xi(s)\): a spectral zeta function encodes its spectrum in poles, whereas \(\xi\) carries the zeros as zeros. hp-4 Theorem 6.1 conflates the two categories.

A.8 Weil's positivity criterion — the exact statement of the wall proven equivalence (classical)

Theorem A.8 (Weil 1952; cf. Bombieri 2000).

For \(g \in C_c^\infty(\mathbb R)\) let \(\hat g(r) = \int g(u)e^{iru}du\) and let \(Q(g)\) denote the right-hand side of Theorem A.4 evaluated at \(h(r) = |\hat g(r)|^2\) (an admissible \(h\)). Then \[ \mathrm{RH} \iff Q(g) \ge 0 \quad\text{for all } g \in C_c^\infty(\mathbb R). \] The forward implication is immediate from A.4: under RH every \(\gamma_\rho\) is real, so \(\sum_\rho h(\gamma_\rho) = \sum_\rho |\hat g(\gamma_\rho)|^2 \ge 0\). The converse is Weil's theorem: a zero off the line yields a test function making the zero side negative.

Li's criterion (\(\mathrm{RH} \iff \lambda_n = \sum_\rho[1-(1-1/\rho)^n] \ge 0\) for all \(n\); Li 1997, Bombieri–Lagarias 1999) is the same wall in a discrete basis. Every surviving route of the series — the kernel route (via A.6), the adelic route (via A.7), the trace route (via A.4) — terminates at precisely this positivity.

A.9 GUE statistics: honest status (Task 6) conditional / numerical

Montgomery (1973), correctly stated. Assume RH, and let \(F(\alpha,T) = N(T)^{-1}\sum_{0<\gamma,\gamma'\le T} T^{i\alpha(\gamma-\gamma')} w(\gamma-\gamma')\), \(w(x) = 4/(4+x^2)\). Then \(F(\alpha,T) = T^{-2|\alpha|}\log T\,(1+o(1)) + |\alpha| + o(1)\) uniformly for \(|\alpha|\le1\). For test functions \(f\) with \(\mathrm{supp}\,\hat f \subset (-1,1)\) this yields the pair-correlation density \(1 - \big(\frac{\sin\pi u}{\pi u}\big)^2\) — the GUE form. The extension to \(|\alpha|\ge1\) (hence to all \(f\)) is conjectural; higher correlations are known only with analogous support restrictions (Rudnick–Sarnak, under the corresponding hypotheses); full GUE statistics for the zeros are a conjecture supported by Odlyzko's large-height computations. hp-6's displayed "\(\widehat F(\alpha) = 1-|\alpha|\) on \([-1,1]\), \(0\) outside" is the Fourier transform of the sine-kernel square, mislabelled, with the diagonal term dropped and the \(|\alpha|>1\) behaviour inverted; and no GUE claim about the sieve matrices can survive E16/E19.

Replacement numerics (appendix N.7): the first 120 actual zeros, unfolded by \(x_j = \bar N(\gamma_j)\), \(\bar N(T) = \frac{T}{2\pi}\log\frac{T}{2\pi e}+\frac78\): mean gap \(1.0003\), variance \(0.124\), minimum gap \(0.387\) — clear level repulsion, consistent with GUE at low height (asymptotic GUE variance \(\approx 0.18\); Poisson would give \(1\)).

5. The irreducible gap

Each of the following is unconditionally proven equivalent to the Riemann Hypothesis:

  1. Weil positivity: \(Q(g)\ge0\) for all \(g\in C_c^\infty(\mathbb R)\) (Theorem A.8);
  2. Hermite–Biehler positivity: \(K_h \succeq 0\) on \(\mathbb C^+\) for all \(h>0\) (Proposition A.6);
  3. Connes' global trace formula in the Hilbert space setting (A.7);
  4. Li positivity: \(\lambda_n \ge 0\) for all \(n\ge1\).

The series' plan — positive finite prime kernels → limiting positive de Branges structure → real zeros — collapses at the limit (the kernel is \(0\), by L2/L3) and at the finite stage (the spectra are confined, by L1). Any repaired variant must inject positivity of type (1)/(2), i.e. must prove RH itself; positivity cannot be inherited from finite truncations whose limit is degenerate. Known unconditional partial results in these directions — the archimedean-place positivity of Connes–Consani, narrow-support positivity (where the prime terms of A.4 vanish), Li-coefficient computations, zero-density and zero-free regions, and verification of RH to height \(3\times10^{12}\) (Platt–Trudgian) — are genuine but strictly weaker than (1)–(4).

Conclusion of the review. Documents hp-3 and hp-7's claim that the Riemann Hypothesis is proved is withdrawn by this review as unfounded: hp-3's central theorem ("\(\Xi\) is a de Branges function") is false for reasons independent of RH, and no other document supplies a substitute. The correct standing of the problem is: open, with the honest reformulations above marking exactly where a proof would have to strike.
