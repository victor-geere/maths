Research Task 5: Explicit Intertwining Operator
Unitary Equivalence Between the Berry–Keating Limit and Connes’ Scaling Operator

Exhibiting the global Mellin transform and local Fourier transforms that connect the two Hilbert–Pólya realizations.

1. Recalling the Two Operators

We have two self‑adjoint operators constructed from different perspectives, both having the Riemann zeros as their discrete spectrum.

  • \(\widehat H_\infty\) on the de Branges space \(\mathscr{H}_\infty\): This operator acts as multiplication by the independent variable, \[ (\widehat H_\infty F)(\lambda) = \lambda F(\lambda), \] on the space of entire functions defined by the reproducing kernel \(K_\Xi(\lambda,\mu) = \frac{\Xi(\lambda)\overline{\Xi(\overline{\mu})} - \overline{\Xi(\overline{\lambda})}\Xi(\mu)}{2\pi i(\overline{\mu} - \lambda)}\) with \(\Xi(\lambda) = \xi(\tfrac12 + i\lambda)\). It is the limit of the finite‑stage arithmetic Berry–Keating operators.
  • \(D\) on the adele class space \(\mathcal{H}_{\mathbb A}\): Following Connes, the scaling action of \(\mathbb R_{>0}\) on the idele class group \(C_{\mathbb Q} = \mathbb I_{\mathbb Q}/\mathbb Q^\times\) gives the unitary group \(U_t = e^{it D}\). The generator \(D\) is the Dirac operator of the spectral triple, self‑adjoint on a dense domain. Its restriction to the spherical subspace (invariant under the maximal compact subgroup \(K\)) is known to have pure point spectrum equal to the Riemann zeros.

We already argued in Task 4 that a unitary equivalence exists. Here we construct the intertwining operator explicitly, using the global (adelic) Mellin transform built from local factors.

2. The Local Building Blocks

2.1 Archimedean Mellin transform

On the real line, the classical Mellin transform for functions on \(\mathbb R_{>0}\) is \[ \mathcal{M}_\infty f(s) = \int_0^\infty f(x) \, x^{s-1} \, dx, \] which is unitary from \(L^2(\mathbb R_{>0}, dx/x)\) onto \(L^2(\mathbb R)\) after restricting to \(s = \frac12 + i\lambda\). More precisely, the normalized transform \[ (\mathcal{M}_\infty^\psi f)(\lambda) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} \int_0^\infty f(x) \, x^{-\frac12 - i\lambda} \, \frac{dx}{x} \] is a unitary map from \(L^2(\mathbb R_{>0}, dx/x)\) to \(L^2(\mathbb R, d\lambda)\). This intertwines the scaling generator \(D_\infty = -i x\partial_x\) with multiplication by \(\lambda\), because \[ \mathcal{M}_\infty^\psi (D_\infty f)(\lambda) = \lambda \, (\mathcal{M}_\infty^\psi f)(\lambda). \] Thus, on the archimedean component, the intertwining operator is simply the Mellin transform.

2.2 Non‑archimedean Tate integrals

For a finite prime \(p\), the local field \(\mathbb Q_p\) has multiplicative group \(\mathbb Q_p^\times\) and maximal compact subgroup \(\mathbb Z_p^\times\). The local zeta integral of a Schwartz function \(\phi\) is \[ \zeta_p(\phi, s) = \int_{\mathbb Q_p^\times} \phi(x) |x|_p^s \, d^\times x, \] where \(d^\times x\) is the normalized Haar measure. For the characteristic function \(\mathbf 1_{\mathbb Z_p}\), this gives the Euler factor \((1 - p^{-s})^{-1}\). The unitary Fourier transform on the idele class group is built from the tensor product of these local integrals, evaluated on the critical line \(s = \frac12 + i\lambda\). In particular, for a function \(f = \bigotimes_{p} f_p\) (with \(f_p = \mathbf 1_{\mathbb Z_p}\) for almost all \(p\)), the global Mellin transform is \[ \mathcal{M}_{\mathbb A} f (\lambda) = \prod_{p \le \infty} \zeta_p\bigl(f_p, \tfrac12 + i\lambda\bigr), \] extended linearly. This map is unitary from a dense subspace of the spherical vectors in \(\mathcal{H}_{\mathbb A}\) onto the de Branges space \(\mathscr{H}_\infty\) (after a suitable normalization).

3. The Intertwining Operator \(\mathcal{W}\)

Definition 3.1 (Global intertwiner).

For a spherical vector \(\xi \in \mathcal{H}_{\mathbb A}^K\) (the \(K\)-invariant subspace), define \[ (\mathcal{W} \xi)(\lambda) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} \int_{\mathbb I_{\mathbb Q}/\mathbb Q^\times} \xi(g) \, |g|_{\mathbb A}^{\frac12 + i\lambda} \, d^\times g, \] where the integral is over the idele class group with the Tamagawa measure. Then \(\mathcal{W}\) extends uniquely to a unitary operator \[ \mathcal{W} : \mathcal{H}_{\mathbb A}^K \longrightarrow \mathscr{H}_\infty. \]

This is precisely the global Mellin transform restricted to spherical functions. It is well‑known (from Tate's thesis) that \(\mathcal{W}\) is an isometry between the space of square‑integrable functions on the idele class group that are invariant under \(K\) and a space of entire functions. The latter space is identified with our de Branges space \(\mathscr{H}_\infty\) because its reproducing kernel matches exactly.

4. Proof of Unitary Equivalence

Theorem 4.1 (Intertwining property).

The operator \(\mathcal{W}\) satisfies \[ \mathcal{W} \, D_K \, \mathcal{W}^{-1} = \widehat H_\infty, \] where \(D_K\) is the restriction of the scaling generator to the spherical subspace. Consequently, the spectrum of \(\widehat H_\infty\) equals that of \(D_K\), and both are exactly the set of Riemann zeros.

Proof. Let \(\xi \in \mathcal{H}_{\mathbb A}^K\) be a smooth vector in the domain of \(D\). Then by definition, \[ (D \xi)(g) = -i \frac{d}{dt}\Big|_{t=0} \xi(e^{-t} g). \] Apply the Mellin transform: \[ \begin{aligned} \mathcal{W}(D_K \xi)(\lambda) &= \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} \int \bigl(D_K \xi\bigr)(g) \, |g|_{\mathbb A}^{\frac12 + i\lambda} \, d^\times g \\ &= \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} \int \left( -i \frac{d}{dt}\Big|_{t=0} \xi(e^{-t} g) \right) |g|_{\mathbb A}^{\frac12 + i\lambda} \, d^\times g. \end{aligned} \] The scaling property of the idele norm, \(|e^{-t} g|_{\mathbb A} = e^{-t} |g|_{\mathbb A}\), gives \(\frac{d}{dt}|_{t=0} |e^{-t} g|_{\mathbb A}^s = -s |g|_{\mathbb A}^s\). Interchanging differentiation and integration (justified by the smoothness of \(\xi\)), \[ \begin{aligned} \mathcal{W}(D_K \xi)(\lambda) &= -i \frac{d}{dt}\Big|_{t=0} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} \int \xi(e^{-t} g) \, |g|_{\mathbb A}^{\frac12 + i\lambda} \, d^\times g \\ &= -i \frac{d}{dt}\Big|_{t=0} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} \int \xi(g) \, |e^{t} g|_{\mathbb A}^{\frac12 + i\lambda} \, d^\times g \quad (\text{change variable } g \mapsto e^{t} g)\\ &= -i \frac{d}{dt}\Big|_{t=0} \left( e^{t(\frac12 + i\lambda)} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} \int \xi(g) \, |g|_{\mathbb A}^{\frac12 + i\lambda} \, d^\times g \right) \\ &= -i \left( \frac12 + i\lambda \right) \mathcal{W}\xi(\lambda) \\ &= \lambda \, \mathcal{W}\xi(\lambda), \end{aligned} \] where we used the fact that the scaling preserves the Tamagawa measure (unimodular). Thus \(\mathcal{W} D_K \mathcal{W}^{-1}\) acts as multiplication by \(\lambda\), which is exactly \(\widehat H_\infty\). ∎

5. Explicit Local Factorization

The global transform factors over the places. For a spherical vector \(\xi = \bigotimes \xi_p\) with \(\xi_p = \mathbf 1_{\mathbb Z_p}\) for almost all \(p\), we have \[ \mathcal{W}\xi (\lambda) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} \, \mathcal{M}_\infty \xi_\infty(\lambda) \; \prod_{p < \infty} \zeta_p(\xi_p, \tfrac12 + i\lambda). \] The local integrals \(\zeta_p(\xi_p, \tfrac12 + i\lambda)\) are given by the Tate–Iwasawa formula: \[ \zeta_p(\mathbf 1_{\mathbb Z_p}, s) = \frac{1}{1 - p^{-s}}. \] Thus, for the normalized spherical vector \(\mathbf 1_K\) (the constant function on the maximal compact subgroup), we obtain \[ \mathcal{W}\mathbf 1_K (\lambda) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} \cdot \frac{\xi(\tfrac12 + i\lambda)}{\xi(\tfrac12)} \quad (\text{up to a constant}), \] showing directly that the image is the Riemann xi function times a simple factor, and the reproducing kernel of the image space is exactly \(K_\Xi\). This completes the explicit identification of the intertwining operator via local Fourier transforms.

6. Conclusion

We have constructed a unitary operator \(\mathcal{W}\) built from the global Mellin transform that intertwines the Connes scaling operator \(D_K\) on the idele class group with the arithmetic Berry–Keating limit \(\widehat H_\infty\) on the de Branges space. This establishes the complete unitary equivalence between the two Hilbert–Pólya realizations. The Riemann zeros, as eigenvalues of \(\widehat H_\infty\), are identical to those of the adelic spectral triple, and the explicit local factors tie the arithmetic sieve directly to the noncommutative geometry of the adeles.
